the logical end-points of <A HREF=\"links.html\">links</A>.\n<A HREF=\"viewers.html\">Viewers</A> must map these conceptual entities into\nconcrete elements within their <A HREF=\"views.html\">views</A>. Anchors are\nthus <I>regions of interest</I> within a view which are, typically, selectable\nor active in some fashion. Anchors are important since they are the &quot;raw\nmatieral&quot; for creating links. Users author links by grouping related\nanchors together.</P>\n\n<P><CENTER><IMG SRC=\"../../images/SphereDetail.gif\" WIDTH=\"535\" HEIGHT=\n\"453\" NATURALSIZEFLAG=\"0\" ALIGN=\"BOTTOM\"></CENTER></P>\n\n<P>As shown above, anchors can be either <I>view-specific</I> or <I>view-independent</I>.\nThe former implies that only the viewer which created the anchor can successfully\nreproduce the intended display of the anchor. The latter are assumed to\nfollow a standardized format such that any viewer which supports the relevant\nMIME type can easily display the anchor as intended. As such, a viewer must\nbe sure to follow the convention of storing the anchors they create in the\nappropriate place; view-specific anchors are associated with a view, while\nview-independent anchors are associated with the view's object.</P>\n\n<H2>Developer Information</H2>\n\n<P>The available operations on anchors are listed below.
