Reasoning group. \n\n<br /><br />\nGraphical models are a widely used framework for reasoning tasks. I am particularly \ninterested in search-based approaches to inference augmented with standard \nvariable elimination and iterative bound tightening techniques.\n<br /><br />\n\nI have worked on exploring the AND/OR Decision Diagram framework for performing \ninference and evaluating its quality on solving high-treewidth problems with \nproblem specific structures such as determinism and context-specific \nindependence.\n<br /><br />\n\nI am currently working on dynamic heuristics for AND/OR Branch \nand Bound. This work aims to find a good tradeoff between heuristic \ncomputation and node expansion in AOBB.\n</p>\n\n            <hr>\n\t    As of December 2012, I earned my MS in Computer Science from UC Irvine.\n\t    <br /><br />Before UC Irvine, I earned my BS in Computer Science from UC Riverside in 2010. There, I worked as an undergraduate research assistant in <a href=\"http://rlair.cs.ucr.edu\" rel=\"self\">R-LAIR (Riverside Lab for Artificial Intelligence Research)</a> with Prof. <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucr.edu/~cshelton\" rel=\"self\">Christian Shelton</a>.<br /><br />I worked on the <a href=\"http://rlair.cs.ucr.edu/ctbnrle\" rel=\"self\">CTBN-RLE</a> code base. Specifically, I implemented the structure learning component, which learns a Bayesian network and continuous time Bayesian network structure from trajectory data.  It seamlessly incorporates any of the inference methods available in CTBN-RLE to handle the case of partially observed trajectories.
