the information \nbelow is intended for general planning purposes only. For previous calendar years, please click <a href=\"http://catalogue.uci.edu/previouseditions/\">here</a>.</p>\n<hr/>\n<h3>\n<p><a href=\"http://catalogue.uci.edu/donaldbrenschoolofinformationandcomputersciences/departmentofcomputerscience/#graduatetext\"><strong>Computer Science (CS) (M.S. and Ph.D.)</strong></a></p>\n</h3>\n<p>The Computer Science degree is a broad and flexible program which offers \nstudents opportunities for graduate study in the full spectrum of intellectual \nactivity in computer science.&#160;</p>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"http://catalogue.uci.edu/donaldbrenschoolofinformationandcomputersciences/departmentofcomputerscience/#graduatetext\"><strong>CURRENT DEGREE REQUIREMENTS</strong></a></li>\n</ul>\n<strong>Additional Information</strong>\n<strong>\n<p>The set of core and elective courses chosen by a student must be \napproved by the student's research advisor before advancement to candidacy. \nFaculty associated with each research area will provide suggested curricula for \nthat area to guide students in their selection of courses. These curricula will \nalso help Ph.D. students to prepare for their candidacy examination (see below) \nwhich must be taken in a specific research area.</p>\n</strong>\n<div class=\"green_bar\"><strong> </strong></div>\n<p><strong>Similarly, students may not take and count undergraduate courses in areas where they have already moved into graduate-level coursework. (The purpose of these undergraduate elective options are to help students to remedy undergraduate background deficiencies).</strong></p>\n<strong>Ph.D. Candidacy Exam</strong>\n<p>The objective of the candidacy exam is to demonstrate in-depth knowledge of \nan area of computer science and readiness to carry out independent research at \nthe doctoral level in that area. The student must complete all course \nrequirements and the research project prior to advancing to candidacy. </p>\n<p>All requirements for candidacy including the candidacy exam must be completed \nby the end of the third year (or, for students with a previous MS in computer science or a related field, by the end of the second year). If the student does not pass on the first trial, \nthey will be allowed until the end of the first quarter of the next year to \nadvance to candidacy. </p>\n<p>The candidacy committee will consist of five faculty members, the majority of \nwhom must be members of the student&#8217;s program, to administer the exam according \nto UCI Senate Policy. Please see the ICS Graduate Office for policies \nregarding the advancement committee membership. </p>\n<p>The student takes an oral exam, administered by the five-member committee, \nduring which s/he is tested on knowledge relevant to the chosen area of \nspecialization. Each area is defined by a set of topics and reading list, which \nare available in the documents below: </p>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/AIMachineLearning.pdf\">Artificial \nIntelligence and Machine Learning Syllabus (PDF)</a> </li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/CompArchEmbeddedSystems.pdf\">Computer \nArchitecture and Embedded Systems Syllabus (PDF)</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/GraphicsVisualization.pdf\">Computer \nGraphics and Visualization Syllabus (PDF)</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/ComputationalNeuroscience.pdf\">Computational \nNeuroscience (PDF)</a> </li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/ComputerNetworks.pdf\">Computer \nNetworks Syllabus (PDF)</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/CryptoSecurity.pdf\">Cryptograph and \nComputer Security Syllabus (PDF)</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/DataManagement.pdf\">Data Management \n(PDF)</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/IBAM.pdf\">Informatics in Biology and \nMedicine (PDF)</a> </li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/DistributedSystems.pdf\">Parallel and \nDistributed Systems Syllabus (PDF)</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/ScientificComputing.pdf\">Scientific \nComputing Syllabus (PDF)</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/SystemsSoftware.pdf\">Systems Software \nSyllabus (PDF)</a> </li>\n<li><a href=\"../../bin/pdf/grad/cs_area_syllabi/Theory.pdf\">Theory Syllabus \n(PDF)</a></li>\n</ul>\nThe field of computer science is concerned with the design, analysis and \nimplementation of computer systems as well as the use of computation as it is \napplied to virtually every field of study and use in the everyday world.\n<p>Computer systems can range in scope from tiny embedded systems to the \ninternet as a whole. Research in computer science involves mathematical \nanalysis, empirical experimentation and the implementation of proto-type \nsystems. </p>\n<p>Core research areas include artificial intelligence and machine learning, \nbio-informatics, computer architecture, embedded systems, graphics and visual \ncomputing, databases and information management, multimedia, networked and \ndistributed systems, programming languages and compilers, security and \ncryptography, design and analysis of algorithms, scientific computing, and \nubiquitous computing.</p>\n\n\n</div>\n</blockquote>\n</div>\n<div id=\"sidebar\"><div id=\"sidebar_title\">More Graduate &#187;</div>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"../admissions/index.php\">Apply for
