<p class=\"section\">Course Description</p>\r\n      <p>One of the main challenges in building intelligent systems is\r\nthe ability to reason under uncertainty, and one of the most successful\r\napproaches for dealing with this challenge is based on the framework of\r\nBayesian networks, also called graphical models. Intelligent systems\r\nbased on Bayesian networks are being used in a variety of real-world\r\napplications including diagnosis, sensor fusion, on-line help systems,\r\ncredit assessment, bioinformatics and data mining. </p>\r\n      <p>The objective of this class is to provide an in-depth\r\nexposition of knowledge representation and reasoning under uncertainty\r\nusing the framework of Bayesian networks.&nbsp; Both theoretical\r\nunderpinnings and practical considerations will be covered, with a\r\nspecial emphasis on dependency and independency models, on augmenting\r\nprobablistic networks with constraints and on exact and approximate\r\nprobabilistic reasoning algorithms. Additional topics include: causal\r\nnetworks, first-order probablistic languages and dynamic Bayesian\r\nnetworks. </p>\r\n      </div>\r\n      <img src=\"../../../%7Edechter/images/black-fill.gif\" border=\"0\" height=\"2\" width=\"100%\">\r\n      <div>\r\n      <p class=\"section\">Prerequisites</p>\r\n      </div>\r\n      <ul type=\"disc\">\r\n        <li>Familiarity with basic concepts of probability theory. </li>\r\n        <li>Knowledge of basic computer science, algorithms and programming principles. </li>\r\n        <li>Previous exposure to AI is desirable but not essential. </li>\r\n      </ul>\r\n      <img src=\"../../../%7Edechter/images/black-fill.gif\" border=\"0\" height=\"2\" width=\"100%\">\r\n      <div>\r\n      <p class=\"section\">Tentative Syllabus</p>\r\n      </div>\r\n      <table style=\"width: 858px;
