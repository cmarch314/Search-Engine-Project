a shalfedge and its incident\nshalfedges, svertices, and sfaces on a sphere map.  A shalfedge is \nan oriented sedge between two svertices. It is always paired with a \nshalfedge pointing in\nthe <A HREF=\"../Kernel_23_ref/Function_opposite.html#Cross_link_anchor_243\">opposite</A> direction. The <I>twin()</I> member function returns\nthis shalfedge of <A HREF=\"../Kernel_23_ref/Function_opposite.html#Cross_link_anchor_243\">opposite</A> <A HREF=\"../Kernel_23_ref/Function_orientation.html#Cross_link_anchor_247\">orientation</A>.\n<P>\n\n<CENTER>\n    <A NAME=\"figureNef3HalfedgeIncidences\">\n    <A HREF=\"fig/shalfedge.gif\">\n        <img src=\"fig/shalfedge.gif\" \n             alt=\"Incidences of an SHalfedge\"></A><BR>\n\n<A HREF=\"fig/shalfedge.gif\">Figure:</A>\n    </CENTER>\n<P>\n\nThe <I>snext()</I> member function points \nto the <A HREF=\"../STL_Extension_ref/Function_successor.html#Cross_link_anchor_1444\">successor</A> shalfedge around this sface while the <I>sprev()</I> member \nfunction points to the preceding shalfedge.  An\nsuccessive assignments of the form <I>se = se-&gt;snext()</I> cycles\ncounterclockwise around the sface (or hole).\n<P>\n\nSimilarly, the successive\nassignments of the form <I>se = se-&gt;snext()-&gt;twin()</I> cycle\nclockwise around the svertex and traverse all halfedges incident to\nthis svertex. The assignment <I>se = se-&gt;cyclic_adj_succ()</I> can be \nused as a shortcut.\n<P>\n\nThe role of shalfedges in a facet is illustrated in \nFigure&nbsp;\n  <A HREF=\"#figureNef3FacetIncidences\"><IMG \n  SRC=\"cc_ref_up_arrow.gif\" ALT=\"reference arrow\" WIDTH=\"10\" HEIGHT=\"10\"></A>\n. The <I>facet()</I> member function returns the facet \nin which\nthe shalfedge is part of one of the facet cycles. The successive assignment of \nthe form <I>se = se-&gt;next()</I> cycles counterclockwise around the facet (or a\nhole of the facet).\n<P>\n\nA const circulators is provided for each of the three circular orders.\nThe circulators are bidirectional and assignable to <I>SHalfedge_const_handle</I>.\n<P>\n\n<I>#include &lt;<A
