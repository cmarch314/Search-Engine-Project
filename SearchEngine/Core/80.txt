The policy also\r\n          contains specific details as to the level of service provided to students\r\n          of the course. This document also details the responsibilities of students\r\n          using ICS provided computing resources.</p>\r\n        <p><strong>Informatics 191:</strong></p>\r\n        <p>Project in System Design, covers the following\r\n          areas of study: Specification, design, construction, testing, and documentation\r\n          of a complete software system. This course has special emphasis on the\r\n          need for and use of teamwork, careful planning, and other techniques\r\n          in working with large systems.</p>\r\n        <p><strong>Facilities:</strong></p>\r\n        <p>Informatics 191: Students in this course are provided use of ICS computing\r\n          resources in CS 193. There are 24PCs and each team can request one system \r\n          be assigned to them.  No food or drinks are allowed in the lab at any time.</p>\r\n        <p><strong>Computing Resources:</strong></p>\r\n        <p>Computing resources for the class include the following:</p>\r\n        <ul>\r\n          <li> Windows 7 Computer Workstation</li>\r\n          <li> Administrator rights on the machine</li>\r\n          <li> UNIX group (gsu) account</li>\r\n          <li> Ethernet connection to the ICS campus network</li>\r\n          <li> ICS Standard Computing Environment</li>\r\n        </ul>\r\n        <p><em>NOTE: Printers are available at the OIT and ICS Labs. Students\r\n          may send their print jobs to these locations.</em></p>\r\n        <p><strong>ICS Support Policy:</strong></p>\r\n        <p>ICS Computing Support Staff will support the computing resources made\r\n          available to the students of Project ICS courses under the policy set\r\n          forth in this section.</p>\r\n        <ul>\r\n          <li> Computer System Support between 8AM &#8211; 5PM during business\r\n            hours.</li>\r\n          <li> ICS does not take responsibility for any data placed by a student\r\n            on a machine.</li>\r\n          <li> No data backup is performed on the computers.</li>\r\n          <li> ICS staff will not install additional software.</li>\r\n          <li> Computers that become corrupted or unusable will be remedied by\r\n            reinstalling the standard operating system environment.</li>\r\n          <li> ICS will reformat the computer hard drives at the end of each \r\n            academic year.</li>\r\n        </ul>\r\n        <p><strong>Students Responsibility:</strong></p>\r\n        <p>This section details the responsibilities of the students enrolled\r\n          in Project ICS.</p>\r\n        <ul>\r\n          <li> Students must backup their own data to their home directory, group\r\n            account directory, or portable medium such as a USB key.</li>\r\n          <li> Students are responsible to install software on the computer they\r\n            are assigned.</li>\r\n          <li> Students are to have all data they wish to keep, removed by 5:00pm\r\n            on the Friday of finals week of the quarter.</li>\r\n          <li> Students are still required to know and adhere to the UCI computer\r\n            and network use policy, and the ICS Ethical Use of Computing rules.</li>\r\n        </ul>\r\n        <p><strong>Additional Software:</strong></p>\r\n        <p>Any software not provided by ICS with the computer is the responsibility\r\n          of the student and instructor to requisition, purchase and install.\r\n          These guidelines are the responsibility of the instructor or customer\r\n          and should be presented by the
