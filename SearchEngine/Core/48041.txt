class=\"entry-title\">\r\n    \t\t\t\tSocial Networking is ephemeral    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>In the Internet World people often talk about the success of MySpace and Facebook.\u00c2\u00a0 These are given as prime examples of the success of social networking.\u00c2\u00a0 These sites primarily cater to teenagers and (early) young adults.\u00c2\u00a0 A close look at these sites makes it obvious that the &#8216;social&#8217; networking there is based on two things: sex and teenage-friendship.\u00c2\u00a0 Both these are essential when we are growing up and during that age (roughly 13-22) those are the two that occupy 90% attention of 90% people (numbers inspired by 90-10 rule).\u00c2\u00a0 What a wonderful age that is!\u00c2\u00a0 But it ends and then one faces the real world.</p>\n<p>\u00c2\u00a0Well the real world is not bad, but is quite different.\u00c2\u00a0 The two dominant &#8216;occupations&#8217; of that age gradually change from sex\u00c2\u00a0to family-commitments and teenage-friendships to professional-networks.\u00c2\u00a0 This gradual transformation\u00c2\u00a0is imminent and is more lasting.\u00c2\u00a0 If the first period lasts from 13-22, the next lasts from 22- till death.\u00c2\u00a0 This obviously does not mean that sex and\u00c2\u00a0friendships end; it only means that they don&#8217;t dominate as do in the first case.\u00c2\u00a0 In fact in many individual cases family and professional activities dominate as strongly as sex and\u00c2\u00a0friendships.</p>\n<p>It is interesting that when people think of social networking they are\u00c2\u00a0mostly\u00c2\u00a0thinking about the demographics that has limited purchasing power and is definitely going to slowly get out of that demographics.\u00c2\u00a0 All of us pass thru that age and then stay much longer in the age that is dominated by family and profession.\u00c2\u00a0 The lifespan addressed by social networking sites addresses relatively ephemeral part of our life.</p>\n<p>Surprisingly, family and professional networking has not been addressed as much.\u00c2\u00a0 Or is it that it has been addressed but has not been successful.\u00c2\u00a0 I think one reason that current social networking has been so successful is because teenagers by definition have more time and energy and are more interested in experimenting.\u00c2\u00a0The real reason for networking sites not getting into professional and family area appears to be that no good ideas have come around.\u00c2\u00a0 So there maybe a chance for innovative approaches to address this need of the society.</p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta-bar clearfix\">\t        \t\t\t\r\n    \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta\">\r\n    \t\t\t\t<span class=\"by-author\">By <a
