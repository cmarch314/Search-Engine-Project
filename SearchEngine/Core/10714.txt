informal pattern\nfor improving a set of requirements (or any other document, actually).&nbsp;\nIt uses conversation as a metaphor for the RE (Requirements Engineering) process.&nbsp;\nIt is an effective technique for elaborating a set of requirements,\nexamining hidden assumptions, and driving a RE effort towards completion.&nbsp;\n</p>\n\n<p>\nThe Inquiry Cycle consists of three phases:&nbsp;\n</p>\n<ol class=\"A\" style=\"font-weight: bold;\">\n  <li> requirements documentation </li>\n  <li> requirements discussion </li>\n  <li> requirements evolution </li>\n</ol>\n<p>\nand requirements discussion is divided into three steps:&nbsp;\n</p>\n<ol class=\"bf\">\n  <li> question </li>\n  <li> answer </li>\n  <li> reason for answer </li>\n</ol>\n\n<p>\nThese phases and steps are repeated in a cycle\nuntil the requirements are satisfactory.&nbsp;\n</p>\n\n\n<h1>Example</h1>\n\n<p>\nThe example gives one pass through the Inquiry Cycle\nfor the Meeting Scheduler questions\ntriggered good requirements discussions:&nbsp;\n</p>\n\n<dl>\n  <dt>What-is</dt>\n  <dd>\n    Asking for more information about a requirement\n    (not a scenario),\n    and usually answered by a definition.&nbsp;\n    Example:&nbsp; \"What is meant by 'keeping participants involved'?\"&nbsp;\n  </dd>\n  <dt>How-to</dt>\n  <dd>\n    Asking how some action is performed;\n    usually asked about a scenario.&nbsp;\n  </dd>\n  <dt>Who</dt>\n  <dd>\n    Confirming who the responsible agent is.&nbsp;\n  </dd>\n  <dt>What-kinds-of</dt>\n  <dd>\n    Asking for further refinement of a concept,\n    for example \"What kinds of meetings should be supported?\"&nbsp;\n  </dd>\n  <dt>When</dt>\n  <dd>\n    Asking about the timing constraints on some event or events.&nbsp;\n    Example:&nbsp;\n    \"When should the scheduler stop waiting for a participant to respond,\\\n    and just go ahead and schedule the meeting?\"&nbsp;\n  </dd>\n  <dt>Relationships</dt>\n  <dd>\n </dd>\n</dl>\n\n<p> Most discussion-phase questions are about\nthe issue under consideration  at the moment,\nbut some questions lead to important parenthetical insights,\nnot on the topic at hand but
