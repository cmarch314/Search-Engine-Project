out the most pressing economic, environmental and social issues facing the world that it feels could be solved with technology within the next two decades.</p></blockquote>\n<p>All these challenges are impressive.  The two of those are my personal favorites:</p>\n<blockquote><p>2.Ubiquitous Computing: Experience, Design and Computing: Making tech fit into the human environment</p>\n<p>3. Memories for Life: Using technology to exploit the value of human experience</p></blockquote>\n<p>As very nicely state about the Memories for Life,</p>\n<blockquote><p>Speaking at the Grand Challenges in Computing Research Conference last year Shadbolt said: &#8220;One theme that has emerged is the idea of a posthumous memory. There are issues around how one interacts and sympathetically records the memories and traditions of people who are dear to us.&#8221;</p></blockquote>\n<p>I strongly believe that in addition to others benefitting from our experiences, our own life will be lot more enjoyable by a system that will help us revisit, reanlayze, and learn from our own experiences. If the system were to force us to take a more rational and objective view of events, then our opinions and prejudices could be blunted. on the other hand if the system allowed us to be selective in which events, we recalled then we could subconsciously select only those events which reinforced our existing point of view, and our opinions and prejudices would grow stronger.</p>\n<p>If humans had to pay for this computing assistance, I think the second option would win in an open market.</p>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t</section><!-- .comment-content -->\r\n\r\n\t\t\t<div class=\"reply\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .reply -->\r\n\t\t</article><!-- #comment-## -->\r\n\t</li><!-- #comment-## -->\n\t<li class=\"pingback odd alt thread-odd thread-alt depth-1\" id=\"comment-2713\">\r\n\t\t<p>Pingback: <a href='http://technology.linkablez.info/2009/03/19/british-grand-challenges/' rel='external nofollow' class='url'>Topics about Technology &raquo;
