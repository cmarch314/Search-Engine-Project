A series of resources and workshops will be offered to the teams throughout the competition.</p>\n<p><strong>Registration</strong></p>\n<p>Teams wishing to enter the competition <a href=\"intent/index.php\">must register online</a> by midnight on the registration deadline. </p>\n<p>Each registered team will receive a 'team number' upon registration. ALL SUBMITTED MATERIALS MUST CONTAIN THE TEAM NUMBER IN LIEU OF TEAM MEMBER NAMES. </p>\n<p>Registration forms consist of a two paragraph description outlining the product idea, contact information for each team member and the area of computing this product impacts. </p>\n<p>Competition details, participation forms, resources and deadlines are available on the <strong>hITEC OCTANe</strong> web site. </p>\n<p>All submissions must be made electronically using the forms available online via the <a href=\"http://checkmate.ics.uci.edu\">Bren School  checkmate system</a>. </p>\n<p>Required forms will be made available at the beginning of the competition. </p>\n<p><strong>Advisor/Mentor matches</strong></p>\n<p>Faculty and industry advisors will be matched with all registered teams. </p>\n<p>Teams are allowed to identify and solicit mentors independently, provided they submit their mentor&#8217;s information to the program administrator.                  </p>\n<p><strong>PHASE ONE: Team building and Idea Development</strong></p>\n<p>The Bren School will kick off the <strong>hITEC </strong>competition at an event in January. </p>\n<p>Competition details, participation forms, resources and deadlines are available on the <strong>hITEC</strong> web site. </p>\n<p>All submissions must be made electronically using the forms available online via the Bren School checkmate system. Required forms will be made available at the beginning of the competition.</p>\n<p><strong>PHASE TWO: Preliminary Product Description</strong></p>\n<p>Teams will work with mentors to develop a two-page Preliminary Product Description (PPD). PPDs must be submit electronically by midnight on the deadline. (See timeline for details.)</p>\n<p><strong>PHASE THREE</strong></p>\n<p>Teams will be asked to submit Preliminary Product Specifications (PPS). PPS should include a detailed description of the proposed product. </p>\n<p>The judges will review all submissions and provide to the teams prior to submission of the Final Product Specification. </p>\n<p>The PPS should be no more than five pages and must be submitted electronically by midnight on the deadline.</p>\n<p><strong>PHASE FOUR </strong></p>\n<p>Consists of the Final Product Specifications (FPS). The FPS provides the final and most detailed description of your proposed product. </p>\n<p>Please limit your final submission to no more than 15 text pages and 10 graphics pages. Submissions must be made as a text or PDF file through the checkmate system. </p>\n<p>Final Product Specifications must be submitted by midnight on the deadline. All teams are required to do a product demonstration as part of their final presentation to the judge panel. </p>\n<p>The date, times and locations for the demos will be announced during the later part of the winter quarter (see timeline). </p>\n<p><strong>No consideration will be given to late entries at any point during the competition.
