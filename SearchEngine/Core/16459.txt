is the TRUSTe \"trustmark,\" an online \nbranded seal displayed by member Web sites. The trustmark is awarded only \nto sites that adhere to established privacy principles and agree to comply \nwith ongoing TRUSTe oversight and consumer resolution procedures. A \ndisplayed trustmark signifies to users that the Web site will openly \nshare, at a minimum, what personal information is being gathered, how it \nwill be used, with whom it will be shared, who is gathering the \ninformation, what options the user has, what security procedures are in \nplace to prevent misuse or loss and how users can correct information to \ncontrol its dissemination.\n\n<p>\n\nThe talk will present an overview of the TRUSTe program as well as its \nrole in providing for privacy on the Internet.\n\n<p>\n\n<strong>Biography:</strong>\nPaola Benassi is Product Operations Manager at TRUSTe. With \nTRUSTe since its launch in June of 1997, Ms. Benassi is responsible for \nthe development and administration of the TRUSTe's privacy seal program, \nand she has been instrumental in educating the online community about the \nimportance of addressing consumer privacy concerns. In addition to \noverseeing the day-to-day operations of the TRUSTe program, she works \nclosely with the Federal Trade Commission and the Department of Commerce \nto implement and advise on real-world applications of emerging policy. \nMs. Benassi has also worked with companies such as Disney, IBM, Netscape, \nYahoo, Microsoft, and America Online to create their privacy policies. \nShe holds a B.S. in Industrial & Systems Engineering and an M.S. in \nManufacturing Engineering.\n\n<p>\n\n<EM><strong><font size=+1>Privacy in Electronic Commerce, the W3C's P3P, and \nUpcoming Technical Challenges for Privacy Protection</em></strong></em><br>\n<a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/~ackerman\">Mark Ackerman</a>, <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu\">Information and Computer Science</a>,\n<a href=\"http://www.uci.edu\">University of California, Irvine</a>,<br>\n&nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp &nbsp \n<a href=\"mailto:ackerman@ics.uci.edu\">ackerman@ics.uci.edu</a>\n</font>\n\n<p>\n\nThe Platform for Privacy Preferences project (P3P) is a new protocol by \nthe World Wide Web Consortium to protect users' privacy when using the \nWeb and electronic commerce. Several implementations \nby major companies are under development, and the protocol is about to \nbe released to the public (last call).\n\n<p>\n\nThis talk will give an overview of the P3P protocol and project.
