\ninformation-centric, the need has increased for graduates who understand\n and can use  technology that gathers and provides information, who are \nable to distill and recognize patterns in that information, and who can \napply those analyses to achieve business objectives. </p>\n<p>The undergraduate Business Information Management (BIM) major \nadministered by the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer \nSciences is a collaborative, interdisciplinary degree program between \nthe Bren School and The Paul Merage School of Business. </p>\n<p>It seeks to educate students to understand and then apply the \ntheories and concepts of a broad, integrated curriculum covering \ncomputing, informatics, business fundamentals and analytical \ndecision-making. </p>\n<p> <cite> BIM for me? </h4>\n<p>Graduates of the program will:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>Learn the fundamentals of information and computer \nscience, including the rudiments of software design and construction \nwith an emphasis on data management</li>\n<li>Grasp the business fundamentals, covering all the \nfunctional areas in the business school, provide a background and \ncontext in which information and its analysis will be applied.</li>\n</ol>\n<p><a href=\"degree_bim.php#startcontent\">&#8593;\n Back to top</a></p>\n<a name=\"get\"></a>\n<h4>What do I take? </h4>\n<p>The curriculum is presented across three general \nacademic areas:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Computing (computer science, informatics and \nsoftware)</li>\n<li>Business Foundations (accounting, finance, \nmarketing, strategy and operations)</li>\n<li>Analytical Methods (mathematics, statistics, \neconomics, management science and decision analysis)</li>\n</ul>\nFor a full listing of courses required for the major, see the <a href=\"http://catalogue.uci.edu/interdisciplinarystudies/businessinformationmanagement/\">General Catalogue</a>.\n<p>Students are encouraged to consult an academic advisor \nin the Bren School of ICS to determine the coursework designed to meet \ntheir educational goals. </p>\n<p><strong>NOTE: Students majoring in Business Information\n Management may not double major in Business Administration nor minor in Management, \nInformatics or Information and Computer Science.</strong> </p>\n<p><a href=\"degree_bim.php#startcontent\">&#8593;\n Back to top</a><br/>\n                <a name=\"what\"></a> </p>\n<h4>What can I do with this degree? </h4>\n<p><img alt=\"photo:: business and technology \" class=\"left\" height=\"140\" src=\"../../bin/img/photos/degrees/bmi_do.jpg\" width=\"160\"/>The major prepares students for a wide variety of careers \nand life experiences.</p>\n<p>Business Information Management majors can pursue \ncareers in the for-profit and not-for-profit sectors or can proceed to \ngraduate school in several disciplines, including information systems, \ncomputing, economics, business, and law.</p>\n<p>The fundamentals of information and computer science, \nincluding the rudiments of software design and construction with an \nemphasis on data management, provide the foundation for understanding, \ndescribing, and evaluating the technology through which most business \ninformation is gathered and presented. </p>\n<p>The business fundamentals, covering all the functional \nareas in the business school, provide a background and
