\t\t\t<p>Barry Diller wants <a title=\"diller News\" href=\"http://news.yahoo.com/s/nm/20061128/wr_nm/media_summit_iac_online_dc_2\">to revolutionize News on Internet</a>.\u00c2\u00a0 He said:</p>\n<blockquote><p>&#8220;I actually think that there&#8217;s going to be real opportunity in the conversion of print journalism to online, real opportunity, which I think very few people have attacked head on,&#8221; Diller told the Reuters Media Summit in New York.</p>\n<p>&#8220;We&#8217;re doing it in our way from an original product creation at this point. I can&#8217;t really talk about what we&#8217;re doing because we&#8217;re, I think, fairly close to announcing it,&#8221; he said.</p></blockquote>\n<p>I like this and I am eagerly waiting to see what he has to offer.\u00c2\u00a0 News has not changed for long time and Google and Yahoo have been remarkably unimaginative in the area of News.\u00c2\u00a0 It will be interesting to see some real out-of-the-Newspaer thinking about presenting News.\u00c2\u00a0 Basically News is nothing but &#8216;report&#8217; of events, perspective on events, and details of events.\u00c2\u00a0 In the current media, borrowing from Marshall McLuhan,\u00c2\u00a0Report has become the Event.\u00c2\u00a0 What needs to happen is to bring back the event in focus with reports, perspectives and details provided to
