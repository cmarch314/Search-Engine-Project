\t\t\t\tACM Multimedia 2006: Day 3    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>The third day at the conference started with the key note talk by Bradley Horowitz.\u00c2\u00a0 Bradley argued for social search and Web 2.0 culture.\u00c2\u00a0 He did make people think about current search approaches in academic community and how those could be applied should be reconsidered.</p>\n<p>There were some good technical sessions after that.\u00c2\u00a0 I attended one where annotation techniques were presented.\u00c2\u00a0 The final session at the conference was a panel on the status of the field &#8212; I was one of the panelists.\u00c2\u00a0 Panel started with Matthew Turk discussing the 2003 Retreat and its recommendation of three grand challenges.\u00c2\u00a0 The three grand challenges were:\u00c2\u00a0 Authoring, Telepresence, and Multimedia Content Indexing.\u00c2\u00a0 All three are important.\u00c2\u00a0 <a title=\"retreat\" href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/~jain/papers/TOMCCAP%20retreat%20report.pdf\">You can read more about the retreat here.</a>\u00c2\u00a0 It said:</p>\n<p><font face=\"NewCenturySchlbk-Roman\" size=\"1\"></p>\n<blockquote>\n<p align=\"left\">The retreat suggested that the community focus on solving three grand challenges: (1) make authoring complex multimedia titles as easy as using a word processor or drawing program, (2) make interactions with remote people and environments nearly the same as interactions with local people and environments, and (3) make capturing, storing, finding, and using digital media an everyday occurrence in our computing environment. The focus of multimedia researchers should be on applications that incorporate correlated media, fuse data from different sources, and use context to improve application performance.</p>\n</blockquote>\n<p align=\"left\">(Disclosure: I was a coauthor of this report.)</p>\n<p></font>As is common, discussion focusses what we are not doing right.\u00c2\u00a0 This field and the conference has definitely progressed significantly in the last few years and is becoming a vibrant field.\u00c2\u00a0 One thing is clear, however, that Industry is\u00c2\u00a0leading in applications &#8212; they are not being influenced by academia.\u00c2\u00a0 I find that academics always talk about that but the academic culture of strong emphasis &#8212; in my opinion over-emphasis &#8212; does not allow them to think of short term applications.\u00c2\u00a0 Currently the gap between what academia does and what industry needs is wide.\u00c2\u00a0 It is nice to discuss how to reduce the gap, but unless the reward system in academia changes, that is not going to\u00c2\u00a0happen.</p>\n<p>Now we go to workshops on Friday.\u00c2\u00a0</p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div
