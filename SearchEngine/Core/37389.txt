Each of these extensions and its motivations are listed\nbelow.\n\n<P>\n<DL>\n<DT><STRONG>Quadruple Bond</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>In addition to ``-'', ``='' and ``#'' for specifying single, double and\ntriple bonds respectively, OEChem also supports ``$'' for specifying\nquadruple bonds.  An example would be octachlorodirhenate (III), which\nis written as ``[Rh-](Cl)(Cl)(Cl)(Cl)$[Rh-](Cl)(Cl)(Cl)Cl''.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Unquoted and Additional Elements</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>In addition to the standard Daylight unquoted elements, B, C, N, O, F,\nP, S, Cl, Br and I, OEChem's SMILES readers also allow H, D and T to\nspecify hydrogen, deuterium and tritium.  Additionally, to support\nSyracuse SMILES, ``CL'' and ``BR'' are considered ``Cl'' and ``Br''.  The\nperiodic table is also extended from 102 to 109 elements, i.e. [Sg]\nfor Seaborgium, with the addition of [D] and [T] representing [2H] and\n[3H] respectively.\n\n<P>\nOEChem may support ``Na'', ``Li'' and ``K'' as unquoted elements to support\nSyracuse SMILES at some point in the future.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Aromatic Telurium</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>In order to support OpenEye's aromaticity model, which allows\nTellurium to be aromatic, the SMILES parser has been extended to\nsupport ``[te]'', such as in tellurophene, ``[te]1cccc1'', which follows\nin the sequence furan (``o1cccc1''), thiophene (``s1cccc1'') and\nselenophene (``[se]1cccc1'').\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Atom Maps in Molecules</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>Traditionally, SMILES atom maps, i.e. [Pb:1], are only ever used and\nspecified in reaction molecules, <code>[Pb:1]&#187;[Au:1]</code>.  However, OEChem\nextends this notion to allow atom maps to be used in discrete\nmolecules.  Hence, both [1*] and [*:1] may be used to mark significant\nsites in a molecule.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>RGroup Attachment Points</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>As a short hand to support specifying templates for combinatorial\nlibraries, and to support existing Cactus and JChem/Marvin usage,\nOEChem allows ``[R2]'' to be used as short-hand for [*:2].  For\nenquiring minds, the SMILES [R2:3] is interpreted as [*:3] or [R3],\nwith the last specification taking priority.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>External Bond Attachment Points</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>OEChem SMILES also allows specification of attachment points as\nexternal closures.  These have the syntax, ampersand followed by a\nring closure.  Hence the SMILES CC&amp;1 is equivalent to the RGroup\nattachment SMILES CC[R1], which is equivalent to the atom mapped\nmolecule CC[*:1].  As with ring closures, bond orders may be specified\nafter the ampersand and before the closure index, C&amp;=1, and two digit\nclosures are indicated by a '%' prefix, i.e. C&amp;%12 or C&amp;=%12.\n\n<P>\nOne major advantage, of this notation is that the SMILES parser will\nfuse attachment points present in a SMILES string, in the same way as\nit fuses ring closures.  Hence, C&amp;1C&amp;2.Cl&amp;1.Br&amp;2, when parsed produces\nthe molecule ClCCBr.  This provides a convenient method of enumerating\ncombinatorial libraries using string concatenation.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n</DL>\n\n<P>\n\n<DIV CLASS=\"navigation\">\n<p><hr>\n<table align=\"center\" border=\"0\" width=\"100%\" cellpadding=\"0\" cellspacing=\"0\">\n<tr>\n<td rowspan=2><A HREF=\"node217.html\"><img src=\"../icons/previous.gif\"\n  border=\"0\" alt=\"Previous Page\"></A></td>\n<td rowspan=2><A HREF=\"node216.html\"><img src=\"../icons/up.gif\"\n  border=\"0\" alt=\"Up One Level\"></A></td>\n<td rowspan=2><A HREF=\"node219.html\"><img src=\"../icons/next.gif\"\n  border=\"0\" alt=\"Next Page\"></A></td><td width=\"100%\" rowspan=\"2\"> </td>\n<td valign=\"center\" align=\"right\" height=\"56\" background=\"../icons/title_bkgd.gif\" colspan=4 class=\"navigation\">OEChem - Python Theory Manual\n<BR>\nVersion 1.3.1</td></tr>\n<tr><td><img src=\"../icons/spacer_1.gif\"\n  border=\"0\" alt=\"\"></td>\n<td><A href=\"contents.html\"><img src=\"../icons/contents.gif\"\n  border=\"0\" alt=\"Contents\"></A></td>\n<td><img src=\"../icons/spacer_2.gif\"\n  border=\"0\" alt=\"\"></td>\n<td><A href=\"genindex.html\"><img src=\"../icons/index.gif\"\n  border=\"0\" alt=\"Index\"></A></td>\n</tr></table>\n<b class=\"navlabel\">Previous:</b> <a class=\"sectref\" HREF=\"node217.html\">24.1 Daylight SMILES</A>\n<b class=\"navlabel\">Up:</b> <a class=\"sectref\" HREF=\"node216.html\">24.
