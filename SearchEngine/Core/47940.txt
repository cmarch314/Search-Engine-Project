\t\t\t\tFacebook in India    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">I was on a very short visit to Mumbai, India to meet my friends Rajesh Jain and Arun Katiyar \u00e2\u20ac\u201c one of those exciting trips in which you go from the airport to a hotel/meeting and then back to airport.\u00c2\u00a0 This was first for me in India \u00e2\u20ac\u201c had no chance to feel that I was really in India.\u00c2\u00a0 Of course a car ride from Airport to any place in Mumbai shows you the diversity that Mumbai and India offers and the day was really hot and humid \u00e2\u20ac\u201c just before the Monsoons \u00e2\u20ac\u201c to make my 5 minutes walk to the car in the parking lot give me good experience of Mumbai\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s weather.\u00c2\u00a0 I did feel that I am in Mumbai.</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">\u00c2\u00a0</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">In this trip, however, I got a chance to hear some young people (three 19 year old girls) talk about what they like and dislike on Internet.\u00c2\u00a0 Their excitement about Facebook was something to be experienced.\u00c2\u00a0 It appears that Facebook has become the most important medium for social communication among them.\u00c2\u00a0 They love everything about Facebook and they are ecstatic about the new application environment.\u00c2\u00a0 They think now they have everything that they need to remain in touch with all their friends.\u00c2\u00a0 They repeatedly mentioned that they are on Facebook all the time.\u00c2\u00a0 When asked what is missing on Facebook, the only thing they mentioned was that it would be great if it were available on mobile phones.</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">\u00c2\u00a0</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">Of course, these people are not your average Indian young people; they are from upper socio-economic tier of Mumbai.\u00c2\u00a0 But one thing is clear, there is a revolution brewing in the Internet space.\u00c2\u00a0 Environments like Facebook and Myspace offer easy publishing tools, powerful communication mechanisms using multimedia experience, and automatically created personal activity reports that are of interest to people in their social life.\u00c2\u00a0 By putting these things in an environment that allows people to know \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcinteresting\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 happenings (yes, I am avoiding the term event intentionally) in their friend-circle, these systems are becoming the most dominant software used by young people around the globe.\u00c2\u00a0 And by opening their system and becoming platform, Facebook has indeed brought in a major revolution in social networking.</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">\u00c2\u00a0</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">One can only wonder what is next in social networking.\u00c2\u00a0 It does appear that the major next revolution in this area could be brought in by putting in natural tools to bring such an environment on mobile phones.\u00c2\u00a0 The fact that mobile phone is the only \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcthing\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 that you can not be anywhere without makes this kind of obvious.\u00c2\u00a0 However, I am not thinking about the obvious and usually short-term extension of the current environment to mobile phones, but a really thought out approach in which features of mobile phones are used to make it a nice client \u00e2\u20ac\u201c as easy or better than PC.\u00c2\u00a0 This could in fact be revolutionary.</font></p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div
