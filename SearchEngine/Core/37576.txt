Both of these functions require that\nOEFindRingAtomsAndBonds has previously been called on the molecule.\nBoth of these functions take the query ring size as an argument, which\nshould be greater than or equal to three.  The definition of ring or\ncycle is not based upon the SSSR, and these return true if there is\na bonded path of `size' unique atoms where each atom is bonded to the\nnext and the last is bonded to the first.\n\n<P>\nIt is often the case that atoms may be in different sized cycles at\nthe same time.  For example, one way to identify the ring fusion atoms\nin indole (the fusion of a five-membered pyrrole ring and a\nsix-membered benzene ring) is to use <code>OEAtomIsInRing(atm,5) and\nOEAtomIsInRing(atm,6)</code>.  Of course, the ``MyAtomRingBondCount'' given\nin the previous section would be a more efficient way to solve the\nsame problem.\n\n<P>\nOEChem also provides an additional pair of functions,\nOEAtomIsInAromaticRingSize and OEBondIsInAromaticRing size, to\ndetermine whether an atom or bond is in an aromatic ring or cycle of a\ngiven size.  These behave identically to OEAtomIsInRingSize and\nOEBondIsInRingSize except that each ring bond in the path/cycle must\nbe aromatic.
