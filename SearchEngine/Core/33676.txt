That\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s a fairly easy case to make if you focus, as Maher\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s interviews mostly do, on oddballs, cranks, and nincompoops: the Puerto Rican cult leader who claims to be the Antichrist, the pothead in Amsterdam with his marijuana \u00e2\u20ac\u0153ministry,\u00e2\u20ac\u009d the misfit rabbi who embraces Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, the young-earth creationist who teaches that human beings and dinosaurs co-existed.</p>\n<p>It\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s also easy to portray faith as a goofy fairy tale if you spend your time deriding tales of ancient miracles &#8212; a burning bush! A virgin birth! A prophet swallowed by a fish! &#8212; but never pause to acknowledge the far-fetched improbabilities inherent in atheism, see <a href=\"http://www.direct.ca/trinity/crutches.html\" rel=\"nofollow\">http://www.direct.ca/trinity/crutches.html</a>.</p>\n<p>Maher characterizes religion as \u00e2\u20ac\u0153fantasy and nonsense.\u00e2\u20ac\u009d Yet atheism is no guarantee of enlightened rationality. In a study released this past September, researchers at Baylor University found that adherence to \u00e2\u20ac\u0153traditional . . . religion greatly decreases credulity, as measured by beliefs in such things as dreams [foretelling the future], Bigfoot, UFOs, haunted houses, communicating with the dead, and astrology.\u00e2\u20ac\u009d </p>\n<p>By contrast, those who reject traditional religion &#8212; \u00e2\u20ac\u0153self-identified theological liberals and the irreligious\u00e2\u20ac\u009d &#8212; are \u00e2\u20ac\u0153far more likely\u00e2\u20ac\u009d to believe in superstition and the occult. Or other nonsense: Maher, for example, claims that aspirin is lethal, doubts that the Salk vaccine eradicated polio, and has praised the horse that threw Christopher Reeve.</p>\n<p>So it is unsurprising that Maher sees only the foolishness and evil that religious people, like all people, are capable of, and misses entirely the extraordinary good that religion engenders. Numerous researchers have found that \u00e2\u20ac\u0153religious people are happier, more charitable, have more stable families, and contribute more to their communities.\u00e2\u20ac\u009d They are less likely to suffer depression or commit suicide, to use drugs or be involved with crime, to drink to excess, or to smoke.</p>\n<p>The Los Angeles Times reported last year on research showing that people without faith were less likely to help a poor or homeless person than religious believers. While both were equally likely to describe themselves as \u00e2\u20ac\u0153good citizens,\u00e2\u20ac\u009d their charitable practices were strikingly different. </p>\n<p>Americans of no faith donated an annual average of $200 to charity; active-faith adults typically contributed $1,500. Even when church-based giving was subtracted from the mix, religious Americans donated twice as much to charity as the nonreligious.</p>\n<p>It is no coincidence that so many hospitals, schools, homeless shelters, and aid organizations have been started and sustained by religious groups. </p>\n<p>We are creatures designed to flourish &#8212; to heal and to help &#8212; when we believe.</p>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t</section><!-- .comment-content -->\r\n\r\n\t\t\t<div class=\"reply\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .reply -->\r\n\t\t</article><!-- #comment-## -->\r\n\t</li><!-- #comment-## -->\n\t<li class=\"comment even thread-even depth-1\" id=\"li-comment-2515\">\r\n\t\t<article id=\"comment-2515\" class=\"comment\">\r\n\t\t\t<header class=\"comment-meta comment-author vcard\">\r\n\t\t\t\t<img alt='' src='http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/ff670bb5d742032545e4c4fb6faef885?s=44&amp;d=http%3A%2F%2F1.gravatar.com%2Favatar%2Fad516503a11cd5ca435acc9bb6523536%3Fs%3D44&amp;r=G' class='avatar avatar-44 photo' height='44' width='44' /><cite class=\"fn\">jennifer </cite><a href=\"http://ngs.ics.uci.edu/rligulous-religion-considered-extremely-harmful/comment-page-1/#comment-2515\"><time pubdate datetime=\"2008-11-25T12:03:15+00:00\">November 25, 2008 at 12:03 pm</time></a>\t\t\t</header><!-- .comment-meta -->\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t<section class=\"comment-content comment\">\r\n\t\t\t\t<p>I saw Religulous and was surprised at how funny it was.
