class=\"section_content\" id=\"abstract\">\n        <img style=\"float:right;margin:8px\" src=\"icon.jpg\">\n<p>Newly developed microscopy techniques are providing an unprecedented look at\nthe biochemical processes that underpin life. These range from cryo electron\nmicroscopy, which reveals structures at the scale of individual\nmacromolecules, to confocal fluorescence microscopy, which can measure\nconcentrations of a particular protein at a neural synapse or across an entire\ndeveloping animal. Just as microarrays allowed the move from individual genes\nto studying the entire genome in parallel, quantitative imaging opens a whole\nnew spatial dimension of gene expression.  Successfully harvesting biological\ninsights from the increasing flood of imaging data will require a whole new\ncomputational and statistical toolbox for dealing with the spatial aspects of\nmorphology and gene expression.</p>\n\n      </div>\n    </div>\n    <div class=\"section\">\n      <div class=\"section_title\">Publications:</div>\n      <div class=\"section_content\"
