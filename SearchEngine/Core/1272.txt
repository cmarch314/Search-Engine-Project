Image\" src=\"http://graphics.ics.uci.edu/drupal/img/2005j.jpg\" class=\"thumbnailimg\" /></h2>\n<h2><strong>Triangle Stripification<br />\n</strong></h2>\n<p align=\"justify\"><span class=\"left\"><b>Overview:</b> Triangle strip representation of a model has been traditionally used for efficient rendering. Vertex caching techniques to render these triangle strips improve coherence in memory access and boost the performance further. A generalized triangle strip is an edgeconnected sequence of non-repeating triangles. In order to correctly render such strips, non-alternating vertices might have to be repeated or &ldquo;swap&rdquo; commands have to be used, if available. Recently, due to the availability of larger vertex caches in the graphics accelerators, remarkable performance increases can be achieved using generalized triangle strips.<br />\n<br />\nFinding a single generalized triangle strip covering the entire model, without modifying the model is NP-complete. Hence, traditionally in computer graphics, multiple triangle strips are used to represent a model. Recent works introduce a small number of additional triangles to find a single strip representation, and hence a linear ordering of the triangles of the entire model. Applications of triangle strip representations include generation of space filling curves and fundamental cycles on manifolds, as well as unfolding of triangle strips for origami. </span></p>\n<p>Publications:</p>\n<table width=\"100%\" border=\"0\">\n    \n        <tr>\n            <td><img width=\"240\" height=\"120\" class=\"thumbnailimg\" src=\"http://graphics.ics.uci.edu/drupal/img/2006c.jpg\" alt=\"\" /></td>\n            <td><strong> Single Strips for Fast Interactive Rendering</strong><br />\n            Pablo Diaz-Gutierrez, Anusheel Bhushan, <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/~gopi/\">M.
