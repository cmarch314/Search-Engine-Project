to reason under uncertainty, and one of the most successful\r\n  approaches for dealing with this challenge is based on the framework of\r\n  Bayesian networks, also called graphical models. Intelligent systems based on\r\n  Bayesian networks are being used in a variety of real-world applications\r\n  including diagnosis, sensor fusion, on-line help systems, credit assessment,\r\n  bioinformatics and data mining.\r\n\r\n  <p>The objective of this class is to provide an in-depth exposition of\r\n  knowledge representation and reasoning under uncertainty using the framework\r\n  of Bayesian networks.&nbsp; Both theoretical underpinnings and practical\r\n  considerations will be covered, with a special emphasis on dependency and\r\n  independency models, on construction Bayesian graphical models and on exact\r\n  and approximate probabilistic reasoning algorithms.
