Study  </h1>\r\n<p>Our lab study aims to examine how differences in national culture impact creativity in teams. We \r\n  will look at how culturally homogeneous and heterogeneous dyads work in creative tasks, \r\n  examining both the interaction process, including moderating factors, and the creative product. \r\n  While many studies have focused on brainstorming tasks and verbal expressions of creativity, we \r\nseek to use more visual and abstract tasks. The reasons for this are twofold:</p>\r\n  <ol>\r\n    <li> much of creativity depends on visualization, abstract representations, and complex \r\n    relations[1]</li>\r\n    <li> this helps to mitigate language factors that play a role in communication of ideas in \r\n      intercultural collaboration\r\n    </li>\r\n  </ol>\r\n<p> The study will present dyads with a complex, improvisational task drawn from the creative \r\n  invention tasks of Finke. [2]  </p>\r\n  <p>In future studies, we will seek to to explore how intercultural distributed teams collaborate on \r\n    creative design tasks. We will examine the effects of collaborative technologies on these types of \r\n    tasks to better understand how interaction difficulties might be moderated and how these \r\n    technologies interact with input and process factors.\r\n    </p>\r\n  </p>\r\n<h2> References</h2>\r\n<ul>\r\n  <li> Sawyer, K.
