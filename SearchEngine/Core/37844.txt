NAME=\"SECTION0028210000000000000000\">\n26.2.1 The OpenEye Charge Model</A>\n</H2>\n\n<P>\nThe OpenEye formal charge model assigns formal charges to elements based upon\ntheir total valence.  In OEChem, this functionality is invoked by the\nOEAssignFormalCharges function.  If the formal charge on an atom is non-zero,\nit is left unchanged.\n\n<P>\n<DL>\n<DT><STRONG>Hydrogen</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence isn't one, the formal charge is +1.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Boron</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is four, the formal charge is +1.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Carbon</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is three, the formal charge is +1 if the atom has\na polar neighbor, i.e. N, O or S, and formal charge -1 otherwise.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Nitrogen</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is two, the formal charge is -1, and if the\nvalence is four the formal charge is +1.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Oxygen</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is one, the formal charge is -1, and if the\nvalence is three the formal charge is +1.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Phosphorous</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is four, the formal charge is +1.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Sulfur</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is 1, the formal charge is -1, if the valence is\nthree the formal charge is +1, if the valence is 5, the formal charge is -1, if\nthe valence is four and the degree is four the charge is +2.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Chlorine</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is 0 the formal charge is -1, if the valence is\nfour the formal charge is +3.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Fluorine, Bromine, Iodine</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is zero, the formal charge is\n-1.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Magnesium, Calcium, Zinc</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is zero, the formal charge is\n+2.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Lithium, Sodium, Potassium</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is zero, the formal charge is\n+1.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Iron</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is zero, the formal charge is +3 if the partial\ncharge is 3.0, and +2 otherwise.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n<DT><STRONG>Copper</STRONG></DT>\n<DD>If the valence is zero, the formal charge is +2 if the partial\ncharge is 2.0, and +1 otherwise.\n\n<P>\n</DD>\n</DL>\n\n<P>\nFor the remaining elements, if the valence of an atom is zero, its formal\ncharge is set from its partial charge.\n\n<P>\n\n<DIV
