Data Type \n<!----------------------------------------------------------------------->\n<H2>Data Type</H2>\nmultivariate time series\n\n<!------------------------------------------------------------------------\n<!  Abstract \n<!----------------------------------------------------------------------->\n<H2>Abstract</H2>\n<p>\nThis dataset contains force and torque measurements on a robot after failure detection. Each failure is characterized by 15 force/torque samples collected at regular time intervals starting immediately after failure detection.\n</p>\n\n<!------------------------------------------------------------------------\n<!  Sources\n<!----------------------------------------------------------------------->\n<H2> Sources</H2>\n<H4> Original Owner and Donor</H4>\n<PRE>\nLuis Seabra Lopes and Luis M. Camarinha-Matos\nUniversidade Nova de Lisboa, \nMonte da Caparica, Portugal\n</PRE>\n<B>Date Donated: </B> April 23, 1999 \n\n<!------------------------------------------------------------------------\n<!  Data Characteristics \n<!----------------------------------------------------------------------->\n<H2> Data Characteristics</H2>\n\nThe donation includes 5 datasets, each of them defining a different learning problem:\n<UL>\n<li> LP1: failures in approach to grasp position </li>\n<li> LP2: failures in transfer of a part </li>\n<li> LP3: position of part after a transfer failure </li>\n<li> LP4: failures in approach to ungrasp position </li>\n<li> LP5: failures in motion with part </li>\n</UL>\n\n<H4>Feature information </H4>\n<P>\nAll features are numeric although they are integer valued only.\nEach feature represents a force or a torque measured after\nfailure detection; each failure instance is characterized in terms\nof 15 force/torque samples collected at regular time intervals\nstarting immediately after failure detection; \nThe total observation window for each failure instance was of 315 ms.\n</P>\n\nEach example is described as follows:\n<PRE>\n                 class\n                 Fx1\tFy1\tFz1\tTx1\tTy1\tTz1\n                 Fx2\tFy2\tFz2\tTx2\tTy2\tTz2\n                 ......\n                 Fx15\tFy15\tFz15\tTx15\tTy15\tTz15\n</PRE>\n<P>\nwhere Fx1 ... Fx15 is the evolution of force Fx in the observation\nwindow, the same for Fy, Fz and the torques; there is a total \nof 90 features.\n</P>\n<H4> Number of instances in each dataset</H4>\n<PRE>\n   -- LP1: 88\n   -- LP2: 47\n   -- LP3: 47\n   -- LP4: 117\n   -- LP5: 164\n</PRE>\n\n\n<H4>Class Distribution</H4>\n<PRE>\n   -- LP1: 24% normal\n           19% collision    \n           18% front collision\n           39% obstruction\n   -- LP2: 43% normal\n           13% front collision\n           15% back collision\n           11% collision to the right\n           19% collision to the left\n   -- LP3: 43% ok\n           19% slightly moved\n           32% moved\n            6% lost\n   -- LP4: 21% normal\n           62% collision\n           18% obstruction\n   -- LP5: 27% normal\n           16% bottom collision\n           13% bottom obstruction\n           29% collision in part\n           16% collision in tool\n</PRE>\n\n<!------------------------------------------------------------------------\n<!  Other Relevant Information \n<!----------------------------------------------------------------------->\n<H2> Other Relevant Information</H2>\n\n<H4>Feature transformation strategies</H4>\n\nIn order to improve classification accuracy, a set of five feature\ntransformation strategies (based on statistical summary features,\ndiscrete Fourier transform, etc.) was defined and evaluated.\nThis enabled an average improvement of 20% in accuracy. The most \naccessible reference is [Seabra Lopes and Camarinha-Matos, 1998].\n\n\n<!------------------------------------------------------------------------\n<!  Data Format \n<!----------------------------------------------------------------------->\n<H2>Data Format</H2>\nThe data is stored in five ASCII files, with each example in the files described as follows:\n<PRE>\n                 class\n                 Fx1    Fy1 Fz1 Tx1 Ty1 Tz1\n                 Fx2    Fy2 Fz2 Tx2 Ty2 Tz2\n                 ......\n                 Fx15   Fy15    Fz15    Tx15    Ty15    Tz15\n</PRE>\nwhere Fx1 ... Fx15 is the evolution of force Fx in the observation\nwindow, the same for Fy, Fz and the torques; there is a total\nof 90 features.\n\n<!------------------------------------------------------------------------\n<!
