 \t\t\t\tEventWeb 17:  Bliki    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p><font size=\"3\" /><font face=\"Times New Roman\">Two important publishing concepts that became very popular in the last few years have been blogs and wikis.\u00c2\u00a0 </font></p>\n<p><font size=\"3\"><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\" /></font><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">Blogs brought journalism to masses.\u00c2\u00a0 Blogs allowed people to express their opinions and share their experiences with masses.\u00c2\u00a0 The editorial structure of newspapers, magazines, TV and Radio stations, and other traditional media sources limited the views and ideas that could be brought to attention.\u00c2\u00a0 There were sections like \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcLetters to Editors\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 or \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcPublic Opinions\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 that were suppose to voice public opinion independent of the Institutional opinion.\u00c2\u00a0 As in many other cases, Internet changed things.\u00c2\u00a0 </font></p>\n<p><font size=\"3\"></font><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">Blogs became popular because Internet allowed democratization of publishing and search engines allowed equal opportunities in access.\u00c2\u00a0 This resulted in a great rise of popularity of blogs.\u00c2\u00a0 Millions of people started expressing their opinions on matters of diverse interests in their blogs.\u00c2\u00a0 RSS emerged as a mechanism to provide rapid access to blogs making their timely availability a major factor in their popularity.\u00c2\u00a0 New opinion makers emerged on the scene.</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\"></font><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">Popularity of blogs started extending to audio, photos, and video also.\u00c2\u00a0 Combined with emerging popularity of iPods, Podcasting and videocasting gained popularity.\u00c2\u00a0 I consider Flickr as a form of photocasting. \u00c2\u00a0\u00c2\u00a0People use their photos to share events and experiences in their life and to express their opinions through pictures and comments on pictures.\u00c2\u00a0 This has become a popular medium to keep up with friends and also find out what is happening through photos.</font><font size=\"3\"></font><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\"> </font><font size=\"3\"></font><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\"></font><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">Clearly blogs remain the most popular of different media to share experiences and opinions.\u00c2\u00a0 But this is going to be challenged by other media.\u00c2\u00a0 Multimedia Blogs are going to emerge as a more powerful form of popular journalism, both at citizen level as well as at professional level, as more popular medium in the age of new media.\u00c2\u00a0 Current blog mechanisms are creating silos based on the medium, which was natural given the technology of the last century, but in the 21<sup>st</sup> century that is neither natural nor practical.\u00c2\u00a0 We will see the growth of multimedia blogs very soon.</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">Wikis has been another very interesting concept that became popular in the last few year.\u00c2\u00a0 By opening the process of creation, one can utilize contributions and refinements by lots of people and could result in better and more exhaustive products.\u00c2\u00a0 The success of Wikipedia as compared to other established encyclopedias is a great example of this process.\u00c2\u00a0 But this is not limited to only encyclopedias.\u00c2\u00a0 People have recognized the importance of the wiki concept ad have started using these as major collaboration tool.</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"3\">Once again, much activity in wikis has been text oriented.</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\"
