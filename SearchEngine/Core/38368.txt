Typically, the number of heavy (non-hydrogen) atoms\nis approximately half the total number of atoms in a molecule.  This\nmeans that if instead of representing hydrogens explicitly, they are\nmaintained as an <i>implicit hydrogen</i> count associated with each\natom, it is possible to halve the amount of memory required to\nrepresent a molecule (on average).
