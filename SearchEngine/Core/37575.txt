Indeed, volumes have been written on data modeling,\nand numerous methodologies have been developed to codify this task;\nobject-oriented design, relational design, object-relational design,\nand so on.  Two measures by which these design-styles can be evaluated\nare (i) how well they can present the same information in multiple views\nor organizations and (ii) the related problem of how well they can\ncompensate for changes or modifications to their schema/organization.\n\n<P>\nThese two challenges form the basis of ``Schema Fragility''.  Different\nusers or applications may require the same data to be organized differently\nfor ease of processing, or reflecting different models or levels of\ndetail, or to provide different user communities with views customized\nfor their perspective.  This ability is not only critical to the\n``applicability'' of a representation, but also its ``longevity''.\nOne of the most important aspects of modeling a ``real-world'' system\nis that both it and our knowledge of it are changing continually.
