your grade a final exam a little development project that will evolve throughout the quarter short paper summaries and class attendance and participation The final exam will entail your writing an evaluation of some sub area of software engineering and identifying what you believe to be promising or at least necessary research directions You ll be able to choose in advance the sub area you ll write about The purpose here is to demonstrate that you ve thought substantively about an area of software engineering to the point where you can identify some important needs trends opportunities insights The purpose of the development project is simply to provide a concrete point of reference for discussion of the various techniques and ideas that will be covered during the quarter Most likely we ll do a requirements specification several designs probably an implementation and some analyses To ensure that the focus of your time is about thinking about the issues and not grinding through loads of detail we ll focus on a simple problem one version of which was solved in the 196 s in less than 8 lines of commented BASIC code yes it is the lunar lander video game Details of this project will be forthcoming The short paper summaries allow you to demonstrate that you ve read and thought about the assigned readings Readings are assigned as shown in the schedule each week of the class You are obliged to write a short summary and analysis of each week s papers The summary analysis should be about 5 words long total per week not per reading I am not interested in reading a paraphrase of each paper s abstract I am interested in reading your assessment of each paper what points do you believe to be the important ones Do you believe those points Why or why not What points did the author s not address that they should have Since multiple papers are assigned you ll have to learn how to present incisive cut to the chase CTTC analyses in few words Your CTTC s on week i s You'll be able to choose, in advance,\r  the sub-area you'll write about. The purpose here is to demonstrate that you've\r  thought substantively about an area of software engineering, to the point where\r  you can identify some important needs, trends, opportunities, insights, ... \r<p>The purpose of the <strong>development project</strong> is simply to provide a concrete point\r  of reference for discussion of the various techniques and ideas that will be\r  covered during the quarter. Most likely we'll do a requirements specification,\r  several designs, probably an implementation, and some analyses. To ensure that\r  the focus of your time is about thinking about the issues and not grinding\r  through loads of detail, we'll focus on a simple problem, one version of which\r  was  &quot;solved&quot; in the 1960's in less than 80 lines of commented BASIC code:\r  yes, it is the<a href=\"lander-1.0.2.tar\"> lunar lander &quot;video&quot; game</a>! Details of this project will be forthcoming. \r<p>The <strong>short paper summaries</strong> &quot;allow you&quot; to\r  demonstrate that you've read and thought about the assigned readings. Readings\r  are assigned, as shown in the schedule, each week of the class. You are obliged\r  to write a short summary and analysis of each week's papers. The summary/analysis\r  should be about 500 words long (total, per week, not per reading). I am not\r  interested in reading a paraphrase of each paper's abstract. I am interested\r  in reading your assessment of each paper: what points do you believe to be\r  the important ones? Do you believe those points? Why or why not? What points\r  did the author(s) not address that they should have? Since multiple papers\r  are assigned you'll have to learn how to present incisive, cut-to-the-chase\r(CTTC) analyses in few words. \r<p>\rYour CTTC's on week <i>i</i>'s topic are due as shown on the schedule.&nbsp;\r</p>\r<p>They must be submitted as follows:</p>\r<ul>\r  <li>by email</li>\r  <li>with the subject line &quot;Informatics 211 CTTC <em>i</em>&quot; where <em>i</em> is what you think\r    it is.</li>\r  <li>with the summary attached as a PDF (Adobe Acrobat) document</li>\r  <li>by midnight Pacific time on the due date  </li>\r</ul>\r<p><strong>Summary of Assessment: </strong> </p>\r<table summary=\"Grading\">\r  <tr>\r    <td>Cut-to-the-chase summaries </td>\r    <td>30%</td>\r  </tr>\r  <tr>\r    <td>Development project </td>\r    <td>30%<br>\r  </tr>\r  <tr>\r    <td>Final exam </td>\r    <td>20%<br>\r  </tr>\r  <tr>\r    <td>Class attendance and participation</td>\r    <td>20%</td>\r  </tr>\r  <!--\r  <tr>\r  \t<td>Final exam</td>\r  \t<td>20%</td>\r  </tr>\r-->\r</table>\r<p> No grades of incomplete (I) will be given for this course.&nbsp;</p>\r<hr width=\"100%\">\r\r<a name=\"readings\" id=\"readings\"></a>\r<h3>Readings</h3>\r<p>\rThe majority of the readings in the course will be papers\ravailable through the\rIEEE or ACM Digital Libraries.
