became the precursor of the ensuing elaboration and expansion of the brain.  The newly created structure, the neocortex, became the largest component of the new mammalian brains.  Neocortex takes up more than 30% of a rodent's brain, and the bigger the brain, the larger the percentage of it allometrically that is neocortex, so that more than 70% of a human brain is neocortical.  \n<P>\nIn the following figure, examples of brains of reptiles, birds, and two mammals are shown (not to scale).
