Yvinec</EM><BR>\n\n\n<P>\n\n<h3>Definition</h3>\n\nA triangulation is a 2-dimensional simplicial complex which is pure\nconnected and without singularities. Thus a triangulation\ncan be viewed as a collection of triangular faces,\nsuch that two faces either have an empty <A HREF=\"../Kernel_23_ref/Function_intersection.html#Cross_link_anchor_221\">intersection</A> or share an edge or a vertex.\n<P>\n\nThe basic elements of the representation are vertices and faces.\nEach triangular face gives access to its three incident vertices \nand to its three adjacent faces. \nEach vertex gives access to one of its incident faces\nand through that face to the circular list of its incident faces.\nThe edges are not explicitely represented, they are only represented \nthrough the adjacencies relations of two faces.\n<P>\n\nThe triangulation classes of C<SMALL>GAL</SMALL> depend on two template parameters.\nThe first template parameter stands for\n a geometric traits class which is assumed to provide\nthe geometric objects (points, segments and triangles) \nforming  the triangulation and the geometric predicates on those objects.\nThe second template parameter stands for a model\nof  triangulation data\nstructure acting as a container for faces and vertices\nwhile  taking care of the combinatorial aspects of the triangulation. \nThe concepts and models relative to the triangulation data structure\nare described in\nChapter&nbsp;<A
