class=\"entry-title\">\r\n    \t\t\t\tSteering Wheel and Search as a conversation    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>I had a chance to hear a presentation by John Battelle at Fast Forward on the last Thu, Feb 8 in San Diego.\u00c2\u00a0 He argued that we need a steering wheel for search (Thanks, John for continuing to talk about that and giving one of my thoughts some credit) and that the next frontier in search is &#8216;search as a conversation&#8217;.\u00c2\u00a0 When I talked about the steering wheel for search &#8212; compared to the current stateless search relying on the &#8216;tunnelvision&#8217; approach leading to the keyword box &#8212; the\u00c2\u00a0 basic idea was that finding answers has to be based on emergent semantics.\u00c2\u00a0 John very correctly points out that in search, it is very important to find intent of the user and it is that intent that also results in focused advertisements.</p>\n<p>\u00c2\u00a0Search as a conversation is an emergent approach to semantics or determining intent.\u00c2\u00a0 It allows the system to understand the intent in a few, hopefully short, steps.\u00c2\u00a0 In most situations one or few keywords in one query are difficult to lead to determination of the intent.\u00c2\u00a0 A series of conversational interactions may lead to better determination of intent.\u00c2\u00a0 This was the basic idea behind Simone Santini&#8217;s doctoral dissertation in my laboratory about a decade ago.</p>\n<p>Steering wheel is a mechanism to implement that.\u00c2\u00a0 Based on the state of conversation, the system presents to the user the &#8216;information landscape&#8217; that it has and the user can than steer in a right direction to explore further information landscape until (s)he gets to the goal.</p>\n<p>I was very pleased to hear that John Battelle is chamioning this idea.\u00c2\u00a0 Search as a conversation will help providing us the steering wheel that may help in making search more focused and useful.\u00c2\u00a0 The real challenge remains implementing emergent semantics through negotiation between the system ontology and the personal ontology of the user.\u00c2\u00a0 The system ontology is usually known, but there is no good way to obtain personal ontology of a user.\u00c2\u00a0 Of course all data mining and log analysis tools are efforts in that direction.</p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div
