The new algorithms exploit the principles of problem\r\n  decomposition using the AND/OR search spaces, avoid redundant\r\n  solution of subproblems using memory, focus on relevant promising\r\n  portions of the solution space using the power of the mini-bucket\r\n  heuristics and prune irrelevant spaces using constraint\r\n  propagation. The AND/OR search space is a unifying paradigm for\r\n  advanced search schemes for graphical models exploiting problem\r\n  decomposability, which can translate into exponential time savings\r\n  for search algorithms. In conjunction with the AND/OR search space\r\n  we also investigate a class of partition-based heuristic functions,\r\n  based on the Mini-Bucket approximation.\r\n<p>\r\n  We start by introducing depth-first Branch-and-Bound search\r\n  algorithms that explore the AND/OR tree, use a variety of sources\r\n  for heuristic guidance and incorporate some dynamic variable\r\n  ordering heuristics. We then extend the depth-first AND/OR\r\n  Branch-and-Bound and best-first search algorithms with the ability\r\n  to recognize identical subproblems and avoid redundant solutions by\r\n  caching (similar to good and no-good recording), thus traversing the\r\n  AND/OR search graph. We also extend all the principles acquired\r\n  within the general framework of depth-first and best-first schemes\r\n  to the well known 0-1 Integer Linear Programs.\r\n<p>\r\n  Our empirical evaluation shows conclusively that the new AND/OR\r\n  search algorithms improve dramatically over current state-of-the-art\r\n  approaches exploring the traditional OR search space, in many cases\r\n  by several orders of magnitude. We illustrate one by one the gain\r\n  obtained by exploiting problem's decomposition (using AND modes),\r\n  equivalence (by caching), branching strategy (via dynamic variable\r\n  ordering heuristics), control strategy (depth-first or best-first)\r\n  as well as the impact of the lower bound heuristic strength.  As\r\n  well, we investigate the impact of exploiting hard constraint\r\n  (i.e., determinism) in the problem, the initial upper bound\r\n  provided to the algorithm, and the quality of the guiding variable\r\n  orderings.\r\n<p>\r\n  In the last part of the thesis we also show how our AND/OR search\r\n  algorithms can be used as compilation algorithms for AND/OR decision\r\n  diagrams.
