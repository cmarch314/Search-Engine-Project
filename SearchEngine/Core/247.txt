It provides outstanding graduate students with three years of support, including a $30,000 annual stipend.\n</p>\n<p>\nBache and DiGiuseppe are among <a href=\"http://dev.grad.uci.edu/news-and-events/news/2012/2012_04_13a.html\">17 UC Irvine students</a> recognized this year as NSF Graduate Research Fellows. They join the ranks of numerous individuals who have made transformative breakthroughs in science and engineering research; many have become leaders in their chosen careers, and some have been honored as Nobel laureates.\n</p>\n<p>\nView the profiles below to learn more about the Bren School&#8217;s 2012 NSF Fellows.</p>\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<hr/>\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<p class=\"image_with_wrapped_text\">\n<a href=\"spotlight_nsf_fellows_2012-bache.php\"><img alt=\"photo: Kevin Bache\" class=\"right\" height=\"100\" src=\"../../../bin/img/photos/spotlight/Spotlight-KevinBache.jpg\" width=\"70\"/></a>\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>Q&amp;A with Kevin Bache<br/>\n1st-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science</b>\n</p>\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<p>\n<b>Where are you from?</b>\n<br/>\nI grew up in Poland, Ohio, and Santa Fe, N.M.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>What are your previous academic degrees?</b>  \n<br/>\nA.B. in Social and Cognitive Neuroscience from Harvard College.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>Why did you choose to pursue graduate studies at UCI?</b>\n<br/>\nUCI has fantastic machine learning.  The weather isn't too bad either.\n</p>\n<a href=\"spotlight_nsf_fellows_2012-bache.php\">Continue reading Kevin's profile</a>\n<!--\n<p>\n<b>What is your current research focus? Why did you choose to go into this field of study?</b>\n<br />\nDoing neuroimaging research after undergrad, I found myself increasingly frustrated with the limits of the field&#8217;s mathematical tools. I started digging deeper into the imaging methods literature and was consistently impressed by the work coming from machine learning groups. I started learning all I could about the field and two years later, here I am. My current research is still in flux, but it&#8217;s rooted in developing probabilistic modeling tools to explore complex datasets.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>What are your future plans?</b> \n<br />\nI'd be happy anywhere that has vibrant people and big, difficult problems.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>Any tips for fellow students who may want to apply for the NSF GRFP?</b>\n<br />\nThe reviewers don&#8217;t have much time to spend on you.  Make sure your essays present a clear, digestible narrative that explains why you&#8217;d make a good candidate. And spend more energy on the broader impacts than you think you need to.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>How do you like to spend your time outside of school?</b>\n<br />\nI have fantastic group of friends here at UCI, with whom I spend most of my free time. I also love to cook, climb, hike and spend time in the woods.\n</p>\n--><!-- --><!-- --><!-- --><!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<hr/>\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<p class=\"image_with_wrapped_text\">\n<a href=\"spotlight_nsf_fellows_2012-digiuseppe.php\"><img alt=\"photo: \" class=\"right\" height=\"100\" src=\"../../../bin/img/photos/spotlight/Spotlight-NicholasDiGiuseppe.jpg\" width=\"69\"/></a>\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>Q&amp;A with Nicholas DiGiuseppe<br/>\n2nd-year Ph.D. student in Software Engineering</b>\n</p>\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<p>\n<b>Where are you from?</b>\n<br/>\nI was raised in Alta Loma, Calif.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>What are your previous academic degrees?</b>  \n<br/>\nI got my B.S. in Information and Computer Science from UCI.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>Why did you choose to pursue graduate studies at UCI?</b>\n<br/>\nI chose UCI because of the high-quality software engineering department (as noted by the emphasis in faculty and research) and because of my advisor, Jim Jones. Other schools treat software engineering as a second-class citizen and subfield of computer engineering (if it&#8217;s treated at all). In contrast, UCI has a Ph.D. in software engineering! Concerning Jim Jones, I felt he was performing really valuable research, and I wanted the opportunity to work with him. Simply put, he does the exact type of research I want to do. Thus, out of a desire to be at a school that values software engineering, and to work with Jim Jones, I came to UCI. \n</p>\n<a href=\"spotlight_nsf_fellows_2012-digiuseppe.php\">Continue reading Nicholas's profile</a> <!--\n<p>\n<b>What is your current research focus? Why did you choose to go into this field of study?</b>\n<br />\nMy current research focus is on automating the debugging process through enhancing fault comprehension, localization and clustering. I attempt to create tools that automatically provide information that will assist developers in fixing their code.  Sometimes that information is natural language descriptions of their faults so they know <em>why</em> their code is breaking; sometimes that information is general locations of the code which are likely to contain the bug so they know <em>where</em> their code is breaking; and sometimes it&#8217;s simply eliminating the noise surrounding their failure so they can better understanding <em>how</em> their code is breaking. I chose this field because everyone who develops software ends up debugging their code, and it&#8217;s often difficult. My hope is to alleviate the burden that is placed upon those who create or maintain software. This originated for me when I was learning to program and would end up spending hours sometimes trying to fix bugs. All developers sooner or later &#8220;bang their head against the wall&#8221; trying to fix their code, and I hope to make the time between those circumstances farther and father apart with my research.  \n</p>\n<p>\n<b>What are your future plans?</b> \n<br />\nCurrently my plans are to stay in academia and become a research professor. I&#8217;m willing to go into industry, but I appreciate the potential freedom that exists (in terms of what I choose to research) in academia.\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>Any tips for fellow students who may want to apply for the NSF GRFP?</b>\n<br />\nHonestly the biggest thing that I would do is to make sure that in your future research plans paper, you give adequate time describing the problem your research addresses. Don&#8217;t be afraid to spend half a page (out of two) talking about the problems that exist in the world or in the research community your research addresses. The more practical and down-to-earth this section can be, the better reviewers seem to like it. Also, start performing your community outreach now! Don&#8217;t wait until you get the grant. Being able to say, &#8220;I <em>already</em> do X in the community with my research,&#8221; sounds much better than saying, &#8220;I&#8217;m <em>going</em> to do X in the community with my research.&#8221; It inspires confidence that you care about the community and that you will actually do what you say (because heck, you already are doing some). Lastly, make sure your three papers tell a good comprehensive story. For example, reviewers should understand to some extent why you are researching your specific topic based upon your personal statement, or understand your past research interest based upon your expected research. All three papers need to tell the same story, YOU! They need to be clear that you deserve the award. In the end, you want the reviewer to say, &#8220;We would be <em>crazy</em> not to give this person the award.&#8221;\n</p>\n<p>\n<b>How do you like to spend your time outside of school?</b>\n<br />\nI am an active member of the LDS church and participate in church-related activities almost every day of the week. My wife and I enjoy board games and video games, and in terms of my family life, my first child (a baby girl) is due in June. I also train with Japanese swords (iaido) most days, am an experienced whittler, and am trying to teach myself Japanese. \n</p>\n-->\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n<hr/>\n<!-- -->\n<!-- -->\n\n\n</div>\n</blockquote>\n</div>\n<div id=\"sidebar\"><div id=\"sidebar_title\">More community &#187;</div>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"../../alumni/index.php\">Alumni</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../friends/index.php\">Friends</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"http://www.parents.uci.edu/\">Parents</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../friends/leadershipcouncil/index.php\">Leadership Council</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../index.php\">News</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"https://ua-web.uadv.uci.edu/eGiving/\">Give a gift</a></li>\n</ul></div>\n<div id=\"footer\"><a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/copyright.php\">Copyright Inquiries</a> |\n   <a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/cgi-bin/phonebook\">UCI Directory</a> |\n   <a href=\"http://intranet.ics.uci.edu/\">Intranet</a> |\n   \n   <!-- <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/sitemap.php\">Site Map</a> | -->\n \n  <a href=\"mailto: i%63%73%77%65%62m%61s%74%65r@%69c%73%2e%75c%69%2eedu\">icswebmaster</a></div>\n</div>\n<script type=\"text/javascript\">\nvar gaJsHost = ((\"https:\" == document.location.protocol) ? \"https://ssl.\" : \"http://www.\");\ndocument.write(unescape(\"%3Cscript src='\" + gaJsHost + \"google-analytics.com/ga.js' type='text/javascript'%3E%3C/script%3E\"));\n</script>\n<script type=\"text/javascript\">\nvar pageTracker = _gat._getTracker(\"UA-605898-1\");\npageTracker._trackPageview();\n</script>\n</body>\n</html>", "id": 247.0}
