type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-experiential-computing\">\r\n\t\t<article>\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t<header class=\"entry-header\">\r\n    \t\t\t<h1 class=\"entry-title\">\r\n    \t\t\t\tEast-West and Object-Event    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>I am still thinking about a discussion I had with John Seely Brown last week.\u00c2\u00a0 I was emphasizing the role of events and experience in emerging computational paradigms.\u00c2\u00a0 And he asked a very interesting question:\u00c2\u00a0 Is this somehow related to Eastern thinking?</p>\n<p>Objects are concrete and Gutenberg&#8217;s invention of the printing press brought abstract thinking to dominant position.\u00c2\u00a0 And this abstract thinking made objects center of thinking.\u00c2\u00a0 With Multimedia coming to dominance now, can we start thinking more naturally.\u00c2\u00a0 To me it appears more evolution of thinking as a result of the technology pull.\u00c2\u00a0 Technology of printing pulled us towards text.\u00c2\u00a0 Now the multimedia thinking is pulling us towards natural varying sensations like audio and vision.\u00c2\u00a0 Text is frozen abstracted version of speech.\u00c2\u00a0 And speech itself is abstract symbolized representation of our thoughts and experiences.\u00c2\u00a0 So text pushed us away from natural expression more towards abstract thinking.\u00c2\u00a0 And that has several advantages.\u00c2\u00a0 Several very important concepts and advances emerge out of that abstraction.\u00c2\u00a0</p>\n<p>Natural has its own advantages.\u00c2\u00a0 I don&#8217;t see any conflict between abstract and natural &#8212; in fact I see strong complementarity and synergy there.\u00c2\u00a0 We need to exploit that.\u00c2\u00a0 And that is my interest.</p>\n<p>But coming back to East-West and Object-Event &#8212; do events are more eastern.\u00c2\u00a0 I really did not think so.\u00c2\u00a0\u00c2\u00a0John Seely Brown&#8217;s thinking was that abstract is more western &#8212; my response was\u00c2\u00a0 that is the evolution of western due to Gutenberg.\u00c2\u00a0He added that he thinks in East people think more in terms of relations and as I was talking about EventWeb, he thought that events are more central in relations.\u00c2\u00a0 And that is an intriguing thought.</p>\n<p>East does consider relations more important!\u00c2\u00a0 Is that really the case?\u00c2\u00a0 Or is it the effect of slower development &#8212; or slower evolution in the last 500 years?\u00c2\u00a0 I see that with rapid globalization the two parts are coming closer.\u00c2\u00a0 Western people talk about Yoga and Eastern people are seeing more individualistic thinking.</p>\n<p>\u00c2\u00a0In any case, this is an interesting thing to think about.</p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta-bar clearfix\">\t
