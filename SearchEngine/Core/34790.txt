 \t\t\t\tEvents: Flickr and Upcoming    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>I heard from multiple sources including Michael Parekh and Bradley Horowitz (<a href=\"http://www.elatable.com/blog/\">see his blog</a>) that Upcoming and Flickr are going to make it easy to post each other&#8217;s content.  Well, that was expected to happen.  It does show the importance of experiential components in Events.  Upcoming seems to be a growing calendar of events (see <a href=\"http://www.upcoming.org\">www.upcoming.org</a> ) and is owned by Yahoo! and Flickr is one of the hottest photo sharing site ( <a href=\"http://www.flickr.com \">www.flickr.com </a>) also owned by Yahoo! &#8212; no wonder they will cross-post content whenever possible.</p>\n<p>This is important that it happened and it is good for the field.  It recognizes that having just calendar of events for popular and &#8216;folk events&#8217;  is not as attractive as it may sound at the first thought.  Eventful has been another company (<a href=\"http://www.eventful.com\">www.eventful.com</a> ) in this space.  By bringing photos with events, the appeal of both increases.  </p>\n<p>I must mention &#8212; in the spirit of transparency &#8212; that I am co-founder of SEraja (<a href=\"http://www.seraja.com\">www.seraja.com</a>) where we are very interested in making events (not only calendar of events) much more experiential.  This site is getting ready to be launched in about a month.  You can visit the site &#8212; but it is still going through many major changes.</p>\n<p>Overall, Yahoo! has taken a good step by bringing these two sites closer.</p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div
