The description is based on\nthe articles [<A HREF=\"../biblio.html#Biblio_em-tdas-94\">EM94</A>, <A HREF=\"../biblio.html#Biblio_e-was-92\">Ede92</A>]. Alpha shapes are\nthe generalization of the convex hull of a point set. Let <MATH><I>S</I></MATH> be a finite set of\npoints in <MATH><I> <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=13 HEIGHT=12 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_mathbb_R.gif\"> <SUP>d</SUP></I></MATH>, <MATH><I>d = 2,3</I></MATH> and <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH> a parameter with <MATH><I>0  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\"> <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"> <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\">  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=15 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_infty.gif\"> </I></MATH>. For <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\">=  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=15 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_infty.gif\"> </I></MATH>, the <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shape is the convex hull of <MATH><I>S</I></MATH>. As \n<MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH> decreases, the <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shape shrinks and develops cavities, as soon as \na sphere of radius <MATH><I><A HREF=\"../NumberTypeSupport_ref/Function_sqrt.html#Cross_link_anchor_1414\">sqrt</A>(<IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\">)</I></MATH> can be put inside.\nFinally, for <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\">= 0</I></MATH>, the <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shape is the set <MATH><I>S</I></MATH> itself.\n<P>\n\nWe distinguish two versions of alpha shapes, one is based on the Delaunay\ntriangulation and the other on its generalization, the regular triangulation,\nreplacing the natural distance by the power to weighted points. The metric used\ndetermines an underlying triangulation of the alpha shape and thus, the version\ncomputed. \nThe <I>basic alpha shape</I> (cf. <A HREF=\"../Alpha_shapes_2/Chapter_main.html#I1_SectClassicAS2D\"><IMG SRC=\"cc_ref_up_arrow.gif\" ALT=\"reference\" WIDTH=\"10\" HEIGHT=\"10\"></A>) is associated with the Delaunay triangulation\n(cf. <A HREF=\"../Triangulation_2/Chapter_main.html#Section_2D_Triangulations_Delaunay\"><IMG SRC=\"cc_ref_up_arrow.gif\" ALT=\"reference\" WIDTH=\"10\" HEIGHT=\"10\"></A>). \nThe <I>weighted alpha shape</I> (cf. <A HREF=\"../Alpha_shapes_2/Chapter_main.html#I1_SectWeightedAS2D\"><IMG SRC=\"cc_ref_up_arrow.gif\" ALT=\"reference\" WIDTH=\"10\" HEIGHT=\"10\"></A>) is associated with the regular triangulation\n(cf. <A HREF=\"../Triangulation_2/Chapter_main.html#Section_2D_Triangulations_Regular\"><IMG SRC=\"cc_ref_up_arrow.gif\" ALT=\"reference\" WIDTH=\"10\" HEIGHT=\"10\"></A>).\n<P>\n\nThere is a close connection between alpha shapes and the underlying\ntriangulations. More precisely, the <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-complex of <MATH><I>S</I></MATH> is a\nsubcomplex of this triangulation of <MATH><I>S</I></MATH>, containing the <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-exposed\n<MATH><I>k</I></MATH>-simplices, <MATH><I>0  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\"> k  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\"> d</I></MATH>. A simplex is <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-exposed, if there is an\nopen disk (resp. ball) of radius <MATH><I><A HREF=\"../NumberTypeSupport_ref/Function_sqrt.html#Cross_link_anchor_1414\">sqrt</A>(<IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\">)</I></MATH> through the vertices of the\nsimplex that does not contain any other point of <MATH><I>S</I></MATH>, for the metric used in\nthe computation of the underlying triangulation.  The corresponding\n<MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shape is defined as the underlying interior space of the\n<MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-complex.\n<P>\n\nIn general, an <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-complex is a non-connected and non-pure polytope, it\nmeans, that one <MATH><I>k</I></MATH>-simplex, <MATH><I>0  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\"> k  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\"> d-1</I></MATH> is not necessary adjacent to\na <MATH><I>(k+1)</I></MATH>-simplex.\n<P>\n\nThe <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shapes of <MATH><I>S</I></MATH> form a discrete family, even though they\nare defined for all real numbers <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH> with <MATH><I>0  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\"> <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"> <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=11 HEIGHT=25 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_leq.gif\">  <IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=15 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_infty.gif\"> </I></MATH>. Thus, we can represent the entire family of <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shapes\nof <MATH><I>S</I></MATH> by the underlying triangulation of <MATH><I>S</I></MATH>. In this representation\neach <MATH><I>k</I></MATH>-simplex of the underlying triangulation is associated with an\ninterval that specifies for which values of <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH> the <MATH><I>k</I></MATH>-simplex\nbelongs to the <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shape. Relying on this result, the family of\n<MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shapes can be computed efficiently and relatively\neasily. Furthermore, we can select an appropriate <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=8 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_alpha.gif\"></I></MATH>-shape from a\nfinite number of different <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9
