class=\"comment even thread-even depth-1\" id=\"li-comment-927\">\r\n\t\t<article id=\"comment-927\" class=\"comment\">\r\n\t\t\t<header class=\"comment-meta comment-author vcard\">\r\n\t\t\t\t<img alt='' src='http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/988c693c2c0fb7dc4d1ba13b10b96be2?s=44&amp;d=http%3A%2F%2F1.gravatar.com%2Favatar%2Fad516503a11cd5ca435acc9bb6523536%3Fs%3D44&amp;r=G' class='avatar avatar-44 photo' height='44' width='44' /><cite class=\"fn\">Bryant </cite><a href=\"http://ngs.ics.uci.edu/negropontes-100-computer-for-developing-world/comment-page-1/#comment-927\"><time pubdate datetime=\"2007-03-14T20:15:55+00:00\">March 14, 2007 at 8:15 pm</time></a>\t\t\t</header><!-- .comment-meta -->\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t<section class=\"comment-content comment\">\r\n\t\t\t\t<p>It was an interesting pleasure to hear representatives of AMD and OLPC speak during 2006.  Different initiatives aimed at closing the global digital divide, providing new educational platforms for the worlds poorest children.</p>\n<p>Last week I was very fortunate to test one of only a few thousand OLPC laptops in production.  Beside me sat a computer scientist, an electronic engineer, and a music production engineer.   We explored the green and white device (now branded XO) I think, I felt its rubbery keys, lost and found myself in its user interface, played a game of musical concentration, and otherwise marveled at the modest but very real achievement.  The engineers went back and forth about materials, architecture, we all kept coming back to the design constraints- invent a $100 device that could reliably be produced and utilized in third world conditions as a tool to accelerate the education of the worlds poorest children.</p>\n<p>The tough delicate green and  white box that I held, swiveling its LCD screen from tablet to standard configuration might not impress me, my kids, or even my kids&#8217; grandparents.  However, changing the frame, adopting the viewpoint of a kid dwelling in the slums of Rio, how marvelous this limited tool could be.  There are many unanswered questions about local training, infrastructure, methodology.   This experiment is an iterative step in answering questions, discovering new questions, and finding a starting point to place the worlds forgotten and downtrodden onto the road of technology enabled education and innovation.  OLPC is an iterative step towards a worthy and demanding goal.  Institutional, political, cultural, and other social artifacts will be far more difficult to transcend that creating a box, applications, and a price point.  God&#8217;s speed Mr. Negroponte.
