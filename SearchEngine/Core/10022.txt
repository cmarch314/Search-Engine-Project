padding: 0 0 1em 1em;\" width=\"200\"/>\n<p>\nWalt Scacchi (Ph.D. &#8217;81), senior research scientist and research faculty at the Institute for Software Research and research director for the Center for Computer Games and Virtual Worlds, received the Bren School&#8217;s 2012 Distinguished Alumnus Award at the 42nd annual Lauds &amp; Laurels ceremony, sponsored by the UCI Alumni Association. \n</p>\n<p>\n&#8220;Walt&#8217;s education at UC Irvine and his subsequent career in academia have marked him as an accomplished researcher, an insightful advisor and consultant, an inspiring leader and a passionate creator of community,&#8221; says Hal Stern, Ted and Janice Smith Family Foundation Dean. &#8220;When you mention Walt&#8217;s name to his colleagues, you learn that he is a tireless worker who contributes unselfishly to support the goals of the research team. He has garnered the respect of all of his collaborators, and his positive attributes are echoed by each and every one of them.&#8221;\n</p>\n<p>\nDating back to his time as a graduate student, Scacchi has conducted nearly 40 years of research that has contributed significantly to the study of open source software development, large-scale software engineering, computer game culture and technology, and social analysis of software development projects. His work combines technical innovation with a keen understanding of the important role social factors play in the adoption of new technologies. Scacchi&#8217;s research record includes more than 150 refereed publications (journal articles, conference proceedings papers and book chapters), and his projects have drawn $10 million in research funding over the last five years, with support from a wide range of corporate and government sponsors, including Intel Research Laboratories, the Northrop-Grumman Corporation and the National Science Foundation.\n</p>\n<p>\nScacchi earned his undergraduate degrees in computer science and in mathematics from California State University, Fullerton before joining UCI&#8217;s graduate program in Information and Computer Science. He completed his Ph.D. in 1981 and accepted a faculty position at the University of Southern California. He spent 17 years at USC, the last 10 as a research professor in computer science and in the business school, before returning to UC Irvine in 1999.  \n</p>\n<p>\nNotes Scacchi: &#8220;It is an extraordinary honor to be recognized with this award by my friends and colleagues in the School of Information and Computer Sciences at UCI.&#8221;</p>\n\n\n</div>\n</blockquote>\n</div>\n<div id=\"sidebar\"><div id=\"sidebar_title\">More community &#187;</div>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"../../alumni/index.php\">Alumni</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../friends/index.php\">Friends</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"http://www.parents.uci.edu/\">Parents</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../friends/leadershipcouncil/index.php\">Leadership Council</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../index.php\">News</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"https://ua-web.uadv.uci.edu/eGiving/\">Give a gift</a></li>\n</ul></div>\n<div id=\"footer\"><a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/copyright.php\">Copyright Inquiries</a> |\n   <a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/cgi-bin/phonebook\">UCI Directory</a> |\n   <a href=\"http://intranet.ics.uci.edu/\">Intranet</a> |\n   \n   <!-- <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/sitemap.php\">Site Map</a> | -->\n \n  <a href=\"mailto: i%63%73%77%65%62m%61s%74%65r@%69c%73%2e%75c%69%2eedu\">icswebmaster</a></div>\n</div>\n<script type=\"text/javascript\">\nvar gaJsHost = ((\"https:\" == document.location.protocol) ? \"https://ssl.\" : \"http://www.\");\ndocument.write(unescape(\"%3Cscript src='\" + gaJsHost + \"google-analytics.com/ga.js' type='text/javascript'%3E%3C/script%3E\"));\n</script>\n<script type=\"text/javascript\">\nvar pageTracker = _gat._getTracker(\"UA-605898-1\");\npageTracker._trackPageview();\n</script>\n</body>\n</html>", "id": 10022.0}
