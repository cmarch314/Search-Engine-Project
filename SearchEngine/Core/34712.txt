<div class=\"wikipage searchable\">\n        \n          <div id=\"wikipage\" class=\"trac-content\"><h1 id=\"PrivacyandNomaticIM\">Privacy and Nomatic*IM</h1>\n<p>\nNomatic*IM changes the privacy landscape that we are used to.  Because of this, we need to make clear what the principles of this software are so that potential users can understand what they are doing when they use our software. \n</p>\n<p>\nFor starters, here are the ways in which your personal information is captured and used:\n</p>\n<ol><li>Nomatic*IM tries to locate you by using GPS, Wi-Fi beacon positioning and network IP address look-up.\n</li><li>Nomatic*IM tries to understand what you are doing by collecting sensor information from your computing platform.  Temperature, sound, light, motion, etc.  Exactly which sensors we sweep depend on your computing platform.  Check the dashboard when you are running Nomatic*IM to get more insight into this.\n</li><li>If you are on-line, this information is reported to a central server where it is recorded in a database at the University of California, Irvine.\n</li><li>The foundation of our privacy statement, therefore, is that you trust us to hold your data.  Given those facts, here are our privacy principles.  These principles apply, unless you are using this software under the auspices of a <a class=\"ext-link\" href=\"http://www.rgs.uci.edu/ora/rp/hrpp/\"><span class=\"icon\">\u200b</span>UCI IRB approved Human Subjects research study</a> which has obtained your agreement to break them:  \n<ol class=\"loweralpha\"><li>We obtain consent through opt-in techniques not opt-out.\n</li><li>We never reveal your exact latitude and longitude or sensor data without your explicit consent.\n</li><li>We never reveal your \"place\" or other semantic context interpretation without first letting you know under what circumstances it is revealed and then getting your explicit approval for those circumstances (in progress <a class=\"closed ticket\" href=\"/LUCICodeRepository/nomaticIM/ticket/68\" title=\"defect: Somehow tell users what is going to happen to their data when they start ... (closed: fixed)\">ticket:68</a>)\n</li><li>Your data belongs to you.\n</li></ol></li><li>Here are some particular ways that we fulfill the principles above:\n<ol class=\"loweralpha\"><li>You may delete your data from our database at any time (in progress <a class=\"assigned ticket\" href=\"/LUCICodeRepository/nomaticIM/ticket/69\" title=\"defect: Figure out a way to allow users to delete their data from the database. (assigned)\">ticket:69</a>). \n</li><li>You may anonymize your old data at any time. This will leave your old data in the database, but disassociate that data from you.\n</li><li>We do not keep track of, store, or know whose data belongs to whom. We require your assistance in order to do anything to your data.\n</li><li>When your context data is submitted to our database it is stored with an anonymous code. That anonymous code is based on your Nomatic*IM software, but we cannot determine which anonymous code came from which Nomatic*IM installation     \n</li></ol></li></ol><hr />\n<p>\nBack to <a
