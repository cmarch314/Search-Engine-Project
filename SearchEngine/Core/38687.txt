Atom, Bond and Conformer Indices</A>\n</H1>\n\n<P>\nOEChem assigns each OEAtomBase and each OEBondBase of an OEMolBase and each\nOEConfBase of an OEMCMolBase an index when it is created.  This index can\nbe used to distinguish one OEAtomBase from another, as it is unique amongst\nOEAtomBases of the same molecule.  Atom and bond indices are also stable,\nmeaning a given OEAtomBase will have the same index throughout its\nlifetime, independent of the reordering of atoms of a molecule, or the\ncreation or deletion of other atoms (with the single exception of the\n<code>Sweep</code> method of OEMolBases and the <code>SweepConfs</code> method of\nOEMCMolBases).\n\n<P>\nNote that atom and bond and conformer indices are not guaranteed to be\nsequential, or even created sequentially, hence atom indices can't easily\nbe used to retrieve all of the atoms of a molecule.  They can however be\nassumed to be dense small integers greater than or equal to zero and less\nthan <code>GetMaxAtomIdx</code> for atoms (or <code>GetMaxBondIdx</code> for bonds or\n<code>GetMaxConfIdx</code> for conformers).\n\n<P>\nEven typing the following idiom may invalidate any chance of support\nand eliminate any glimmer of respect from OpenEye Scientific Software\nor the computational chemistry/chemoinformatics community.\n\n<P>\n<dl><dd><pre class=\"verbatim\">// Never ever, ever do this!\nOEAtomBase *aptr;\nunsigned int i;\n\nfor (i=0; i&lt;mol.NumAtoms(); ++i)\n{\n  aptr = mol.GetAtom(OEHasAtomIdx(i));\n  // Pretend that aptr is valid\n}\n</pre></dl>\n\n<P>\nThere are far more efficient methods of crashing computer software\nthat should be used instead.
