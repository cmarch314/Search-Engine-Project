Embryonic Stem Cell-derived Heart Cells</div>\n    <div id=\"paper_authors\">Jesus&nbsp;Luna, Jesus&nbsp;Ciriza, Marcos&nbsp;Ojeda-Garcia, Marco&nbsp;Kong, Anthony&nbsp;Herren, Deborah&nbsp;Lieu, Ronald&nbsp;Li, <a href=\"/people/23.html\">Charless&nbsp;Fowlkes</a>, Michelle&nbsp;Khine, Kara&nbsp;McCloskey</div>\n    <div id=\"abstract\">\n      <img style=\"float:left;\" src=\"icon_drop.jpg\" alt=\"icon\">\n      Nano- and microscale topographical cues play critical roles in the induction and maintenance of various cellular\nfunctions, including morphology, adhesion, gene regulation, and communication. Recent studies indicate that\nstructure and function at the heart tissue level is exquisitely sensitive to mechanical cues at the nano-scale as well\nas at the microscale level. Although fabrication methods exist for generating topographical features for cell\nculture, current techniques, especially those with nanoscale resolution, are typically complex, prohibitively\nexpensive, and not accessible to most biology laboratories. Here, we present a tunable culture platform comprised\nof biomimetic wrinkles that simulate the heart's complex anisotropic and multiscale architecture for facile\nand robust cardiac cell alignment. We demonstrate the cellular and subcellular alignment of both neonatal\nmouse cardiomyocytes as well as those derived from human embryonic stem cells. By mimicking the fibrillar\nnetwork of the extracellular matrix, this system enables monitoring of protein localization in real time and\ntherefore the high-resolution study of phenotypic and physiologic responses to in-vivo like topographical cues.\n\n    </div>\n    <div id=\"bibtext\">\n      <h3>Download: <a href=\"/papers/Luna_TEC_2011/Luna_TEC_2011.pdf\">pdf</a></h3>\n      <h3>Text Reference</h3>\nJesus Luna, Jesus Ciriza, Marcos Ojeda-Garcia, Marco Kong, Anthony Herren, Deborah Lieu, Ronald Li, Charless Fowlkes, Michelle Khine, and Kara McCloskey.\nMulti-scale biomimetic topography for the alignment of neonatal and embryonic stem cell-derived heart cells.\n<em>Tissue Engineering: Part C</em>, 2011.<br>\n<h3>BibTeX Reference</h3>\n@article{<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Luna_TEC_2011,<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;author = \"Luna, Jesus and Ciriza, Jesus and Ojeda-Garcia, Marcos and Kong, Marco and Herren, Anthony and Lieu, Deborah and Li, Ronald and Fowlkes, Charless and Khine, Michelle and McCloskey, Kara\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;journal = \"Tissue Engineering: Part C\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;title = \"Multi-scale Biomimetic Topography for the Alignment of Neonatal and Embryonic Stem Cell-derived Heart Cells\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;year = \"2011\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;volume = \"17(5)\"<br>\n}<br>\n<br>\n    </div>\n  </div>\n  <div id=\"footer\">\n    <div>\n      <a href=\"/\">Computational Vision</a> |\n      <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/\">School of Information and Computer Sciences</a> |\n      <a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/\">UC Irvine</a>\n    </div>\n    <div id=\"updated\">&copy; 2007-2015 UC Irvine</div>\n  </div>\n  </div>\n</body>\n</html>\n\n", "id": 4970.0}
