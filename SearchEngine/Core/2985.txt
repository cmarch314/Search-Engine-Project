style=\"float:left;\" src=\"icon_drop.jpg\" alt=\"icon\">\n          We present a model of curvilinear grouping using piecewise linear\nrepresentations of contours and a conditional random field to capture\ncontinuity and the frequency of different junction types. Potential completions\nare generated by building a constrained Delaunay triangulation (CDT) over the\nset of contours found by a local edge detector.\n\n    Maximum likelihood parameters for the model are learned from human labeled\ngroundtruth. Using held out test data, we measure how the model, by\nincorporating continuity structure, improves boundary detection over the local\nedge detector. We also compare performance with a baseline local classifier\nthat operates on pairs of edgels.\n\n    Both algorithms consistently dominate the low-level boundary detector at\nall thresholds. To our knowledge, this is the first time that curvilinear\ncontinuity has been shown quantitatively useful for a large variety of natural\nimages.  Better boundary detection has immediate application in the problem of\nobject detection and recognition.\n\n\n    </div>\n    <div id=\"bibtext\">\n      <h3>Download: <a href=\"/papers/RenFM_ICCV_2005/RenFM_ICCV_2005.pdf\">pdf</a></h3>\n      <h3>Text Reference</h3>\nXiaofeng Ren, Charless&nbsp;C.
