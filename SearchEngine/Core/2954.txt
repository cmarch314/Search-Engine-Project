Model</div>\n    <div id=\"paper_authors\">Pedro&nbsp;Felzenszwalb, David&nbsp;McAllester, <a href=\"/people/20.html\">Deva&nbsp;Ramanan</a></div>\n    <div id=\"abstract\">\n      <img style=\"float:left;\" src=\"icon_drop.jpg\" alt=\"icon\">\n      This paper describes a discriminatively trained, multiscale,\ndeformable part model for object detection. Our system\nachieves a two-fold improvement in average precision\nover the best performance in the 2006 PASCAL person detection\nchallenge. It also outperforms the best results in the\n2007 challenge in ten out of twenty categories. The system\nrelies heavily on deformable parts. While deformable part\nmodels have become quite popular, their value had not been\ndemonstrated on difficult benchmarks such as the PASCAL\nchallenge. Our system also relies heavily on new methods\nfor discriminative training. We combine a margin-sensitive\napproach for data mining hard negative examples with a\nformalism we call latent SVM. A latent SVM, like a hidden\nCRF, leads to a non-convex training problem. However,\na latent SVM is semi-convex and the training problem\nbecomes convex once latent information is specified for\nthe positive examples. We believe that our training methods\nwill eventually make possible the effective use of more\nlatent information such as hierarchical (grammar) models\nand models involving latent three dimensional pose.\n\n    </div>\n    <div id=\"bibtext\">\n      <h3>Download: <a href=\"/papers/FelzenszwalbMR_CVPR_2008/FelzenszwalbMR_CVPR_2008.pdf\">pdf</a></h3>\n      <h3>Text Reference</h3>\nPedro Felzenszwalb, David McAllester, and Deva Ramanan.\nA discriminatively trained, multiscale, deformable part model.\nIn <em>IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR)</em>. 2008.<br>\n<h3>BibTeX Reference</h3>\n@inproceedings{<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;FelzenszwalbMR_CVPR_2008,<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;author = \"Felzenszwalb, Pedro and McAllester, David and Ramanan, Deva\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;title = \"A Discriminatively Trained, Multiscale, Deformable Part Model\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;year = \"2008\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;booktitle = \"IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR)\"<br>\n}<br>\n<br>\n    </div>\n  </div>\n  <div id=\"footer\">\n    <div>\n      <a href=\"/\">Computational Vision</a> |\n      <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/\">School of Information and Computer Sciences</a> |\n      <a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/\">UC Irvine</a>\n    </div>\n    <div id=\"updated\">&copy; 2007-2015 UC Irvine</div>\n  </div>\n  </div>\n</body>\n</html>\n\n", "id": 2954.0}
