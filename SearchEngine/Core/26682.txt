continue to retain an effective process focus \nis a challenge that may best be met at the \"grass roots\" level through \ncollegial teams working to maintain their culture. This presentation \ndefines the use of teams from multiple projects to define and implement \na strong process improvement program that provides continuity over time. \nTeam involvement in establishment of goals, development of training to \nsupport the process, evaluation of project metrics, and sharing lessons \nlearned, all establish a culture of sharing that helps prevent \ndeterioration when there are changes in management and new challenges \nin the marketplace.\n\n<p>\n\nExamples from process programs in place within various organizations are \nincluded, with emphasis on the Command and Control Systems (CCS) division \nof Raytheon Systems Company (formerly Hughes Aircraft Company). In this \ndivision a long-established culture of planning and managing software \nprojects has led to a shared understanding of how projects \"should\" be \norganized and managed for greater effectiveness. The ongoing use of \ncollegial teams to discuss process issues and lessons learned, propose \nsolutions shared between projects, and implement process initiatives \nhas proven to be successful in obtaining buy-in and enthusiasm.\n\n<p>\n\n<strong>Biography: </strong>\nAs Technical Director of the Command and Control Systems \nDivision Engineering Process Group (EPG), Jane Moon initiated and led her \ndivision's improvement effort from SEI CMM Level 2 to its current Level 4 \nand 5 activities as a designated center of excellence for software \nengineering within Raytheon. Ms. Moon played a key role in defining the \norganization and focus of EPGs within Hughes Aircraft Company and the \ncompany-level process deployment approach. She has mentored process group \npersonnel in many organizations. Jane has been a member of the CMM \nIntegration (CMMI) Project, a key reviewer of both the software CMM and \nsystems engineering CMM, and was a member of the SCE and CBA Advisory \nBoards to the SEI. She is a qualified Lead Assessor of the Internal \nProcess Improvement (IPI) assessment method. She has been a presenter \nat the SEPG National Conference, SEI Symposium, SEI Risk Management \nConference, and other conferences and process-related events. Ms. Moon \nhas 30 years of software development and management experience, with the past \n17 years at Raytheon.
