Navigation Panel-->\n\n<H2><A NAME=\"SECTION035110000000000000000\">\nA.1.1 OEParseIUPACName Improvements</A>\n</H2>\nSupport for parsing substituents (name fragments) by generating a wildcard\natom in SMILES, for example, ``methyl'' returns <code>*C</code> and ``hydroxy''\nreturns <code>*O</code>.\nAdds support for parenthesized indicated hydrogens, <I>e.g.</I>\n``naphthalen-1(2H)-one''.\nTweaks to support names, such as ``9,10-anthracene-dicarboxylic acid'',\nwhere the hyphen after the stem would cause the parser to expect a list\nof locants.\nFixes to multipliers of ``oxy'' (and related) linkers, so that\n``dineopentyloxybenzene'' is correctly considered two copies of the\nprefix ``neopentoxy''.\nAdded support for ``...ic aldehyde'' and ``..ic acid aldehyde'' as\nsynonyms of ``...aldehyde''.  Added support for both ``yl'' and ``oyl''\nvariants of traditional acid stems, <I>i.e.</I> both ``crontonoyl'' and\n``crotonyl'', and both ``acryloyl'' and ``acrylyl''.  Adds support for\n``...amido'' as a traditional linker, <I>e.g.</I> ``propanamido'',\n``acrylamido'' and ``benzamido''.\nAdded support for traditional bivalent imides, <I>e.g.</I> ``succinimide'',\nincluding N-substituted forms, <I>e.g.</I> ``N-iodosuccinimide''.\nFixes parsing of salt multipliers, <I>e.g.</I> ``benzene trihydrate''.\nFixes nitrogenous linker processing, <I>e.g.</I> ``N,N-dimethylsulfamoyl\nchloride''.\nAdds support for locants alpha, beta and gamma (as written in english),\nallowing us to handle ``alpha,alpha,alpha-trichlorotoluene'' and\n``beta-mercaptoethanol''.\nImprovements to handle unspecified locants, <I>e.g.</I> ``chlorophenyl''\nand ``pentachlorophenyl''.\nSupport for substitutions on ``benzoyl'' and ``benzamido'' prefixes,\n<I>e.g.</I> ``4-nitrobenzoyl chloride''.\nHandling of names using di, tri etc. incorrectly when bis, tris etc.\nshould have been used, <I>e.g.</I> ``2,4-di(t-butylperoxy)hexane''.\nImprovements to ring locant numbers on ``benzophenone'' and ``benzidine'',\nand support for primed locants on ring system parents.\nAdds N and N' locants to the hydrazine stem, <I>e.g.</I>\n``4-(N',N'-dihydroxyhydrazino)benzoic acid''.\n\n<P>\nTreat ``imidamide'' as a synonym for ``amidine'', and ``hydrazinyl''\nas a synonym for ``hydrazino''.\n\n<P>\nAdds suppprt for the element ``columbium'' (more commonly known as\n``niobium'').\n\n<P>\nAdds support for the prefixes ``keto'' (synonym for ``oxo''),\n``allophanoyl'', ``hydantoyl'', ``ureido'', ``carbamido'', ``lauryl'',\n``myrsityl'', ``palmityl'', ``stearyl', ``carbamimidoyl'',\n``sulfinamoyl'', ``thiocarbamoyl'', ``carbamothioyl'', ``carbamoyl'',\n``guanyl'', ``morpholino'', ``oxycyano'', ``sulfinyl'', ``sulfonyl''\nand ``fulminato''.\n\n<P>\nAdded support for ``acetonyl'' and ``phenacyl'' as vinyl-like prefixes,\nincluding their ``...idene'' and ``..idyne'' variants.\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the traditional stems ``vanillic acid'', ``isovanillic\nacid'', ``syringic acid'', ``arachidic acid'', ``behenic acid'',\n``carboceric acid'', ``cerinic acid'', ``ceromelissic acid'',\n``ceroplastic acid'', ``cerotic acid'', ``daturic acid'', ``enanthic acid'',\n``geddic acid'', ``gheddic acid'', ``japanic acid'', ``lacceric acid'',\n``lignoceric acid'', ``margaric acid'', ``melissic acid'', ``montanic\nacid'', ``pelargonic acid'', ``psylic acid'', ``thapsic acid'',\n``brassylic acid'' and ``pyruvic acid''.\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the stems ``ketene'', ``thioketene'', ``vanillin'',\n``isovanillin'', ``rhodanine'', ``allophanic acid'', ``hydantoic acid'',\n``picoline'', ``borate'' <code>[BH4-]</code>, ``fulvene'', ``isobutene'',\n``isoprene'', ``alloxane'', ``barbituric acid'', ``hydantoin'',\n``cytosine'', ``guanine'', ``hydroxylamine'' and the common amino acids.\n\n<P>\nAdds support for the ring systems ``benzimidazole'', ``benzoimidazole'',\n``benzothiophene'', ``benzoxazole'', ``benzooxazole'', ``benzothiazole'',\n``benzotriazole'', ``benzotrioxazole'', ``pyrene'', ``perylene'',\n``as-indacene'', ``s-indacene'', ``pyrrolizine'' and ``quinolizine''.\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the ring suffix ``carbonyl chloride'' (and other\nacid halides).\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the suffixes ``thioketone'', ``sulfide'', ``nitrite'',\n``azanium'', ``thial'', ``diazonium'', ``arsonic acid'', ``peroxoic\nacid'', ``carboperoxoic acid'', ``carbothioamide'', ``carboximidamide'',\n``carboxamidine'', ``aldehyde oxime'' ``one oxime''.\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the linkers ``mercuri'', ``carbamimidoyl'', ``sulfinamoyl'',\n``sulfamoyl'', ``thiocarbamoyl'', ``carbamothioyl'', ``carbonimidoyl'',\nand ``thioyl'' (as in ``ethanethioylbenzene'').\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the salts ``hydrobromide'', ``hydrofluoride'',\n``hydroiodide'', ``triiodide'', ``hydrotriiode'', ``sulfite'',\n``peroxide'', ``perchlorate'', ``perbromate'', ``periodate'',\n``hydrate'', ``nitrite'', ``hypochlorite'', ``chlorite'', ``chlorate'',\n``bromate'', ``iodate'', ``nitrate'', ``cyanide'' and ``cyanate''\n(including iso and thio variants).\n\n<P>\nAdded support for ``hydrochloric acid'', ``hydrobromic acid'',\n``hydrofluoric acid'', ``hydroiodic acid'', ``hydrotriiodic acid'',\nand ``tetronic acid''.\n\n<P>\n\n<DIV
