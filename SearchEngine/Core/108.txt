which\nprovides the standard nomenclature for the entire computer science field &#8212; it sought\nexperts from academia and industry to serve as team leaders for this\nchallenging and long-overdue task. The organization tapped Gene Tsudik, director\nof UCI&#8217;s Secure Computing and Networking Center and of the interdisciplinary\nNetworked Systems graduate program, with overseeing a brand-new section on Security\nand Privacy. </p>\n<p>ACM introduced its classification system in 1964 and has not\npublished an update since 1998. &#8220;The terminology had become archaic and new fields have emerged\nduring the past 15 years,&#8221; notes Tsudik. &#8220;For instance, cloud computing did not\nexist at all, and security and privacy was a fringe element back then.&#8221;</p>\n<p>Tsudik &#8212; who also serves as editor-in-chief\nof the ACM <i>Transactions on Information and System Security</i> (TISSEC), a leading\narchival research journal covering computer/network/information security and\nprivacy &#8212; recruited Columbia University professor Angelos\nKeromytis to collaborate in leading a team of half a dozen prominent researchers\nto serve as a mini-editorial board. From late 2010 until about two years later,\nthe all-volunteer group composed and vetted the complex list, which encompasses\nsuch heterogeneous categories as cryptography, intrusion detection and malware\nmitigation, as well as security in hardware, software and applications,\ndatabase/storage, networks, services and systems.</p>\n<p>Tsudik and Keromytis also collaborated with other team leaders\nin the overarching ACM project to ensure consistent terminology throughout the\nrevised classification system, and to minimize overlap between categories. </p>\n<p>The final product is a specification system that helps authors\nappropriately label their articles with keywords that accurately reflect the\ntopic, explains Tsudik. And that means anyone doing research in the field will have\nan easier time locating needed information.</p>\n<p>&#8220;All documents today are digitized &#8212; from journal articles to\nconference proceedings &#8212; so consistent terminology is important,&#8221; says Tsudik,\nwho was one of only 17 team leaders worldwide on the entire project. &#8220;It&#8217;s like\nTwitter and hashtags. It allows you to conduct searches more effectively.&#8221;</p>\n<p>The new ACM taxonomy may be viewed at <a href=\"http://dl.acm.org/ccs.cfm\">http://dl.acm.org/ccs.cfm</a>.</p>\n\n\n</div>\n</blockquote>\n</div>\n<div id=\"sidebar\"><div id=\"sidebar_title\">More community &#187;</div>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"../../alumni/index.php\">Alumni</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../friends/index.php\">Friends</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"http://www.parents.uci.edu/\">Parents</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../../friends/leadershipcouncil/index.php\">Leadership Council</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../index.php\">News</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"https://ua-web.uadv.uci.edu/eGiving/\">Give a gift</a></li>\n</ul></div>\n<div id=\"footer\"><a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/copyright.php\">Copyright Inquiries</a> |\n   <a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/cgi-bin/phonebook\">UCI Directory</a> |\n   <a href=\"http://intranet.ics.uci.edu/\">Intranet</a> |\n   \n   <!-- <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/sitemap.php\">Site Map</a> | -->\n \n  <a href=\"mailto: i%63%73%77%65%62m%61s%74%65r@%69c%73%2e%75c%69%2eedu\">icswebmaster</a></div>\n</div>\n<script type=\"text/javascript\">\nvar gaJsHost = ((\"https:\" == document.location.protocol) ? \"https://ssl.\" : \"http://www.\");\ndocument.write(unescape(\"%3Cscript src='\" + gaJsHost + \"google-analytics.com/ga.js' type='text/javascript'%3E%3C/script%3E\"));\n</script>\n<script type=\"text/javascript\">\nvar pageTracker = _gat._getTracker(\"UA-605898-1\");\npageTracker._trackPageview();\n</script>\n</body>\n</html>", "id": 108.0}
