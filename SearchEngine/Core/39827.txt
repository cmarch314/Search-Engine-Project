SpatialSeparator>\"></A>\n<h2><I>CGAL::Sliding_fair&lt;Traits, SpatialSeparator&gt;</I></h2>\n<P>\n\n<H3>Definition</H3>Implements the <I>sliding fair</I> splitting rule.\nThis splitting rule is a compromise between the <I><A HREF=\"FunctionObjectClass_Fair.html#Cross_link_anchor_1282\">Fair</A></I> splitting rule\nand the <I><A HREF=\"FunctionObjectClass_Sliding_midpoint.html#Cross_link_anchor_1328\">Sliding_midpoint</A></I> rule.  Sliding fair-split is based on the\ntheory that there are two types of splits that are good: balanced\nsplits that produce fat rectangles, and unbalanced splits provided the\nrectangle with fewer points is fat.\n<P>\n\nAlso, this splitting rule maintains an upper bound on the maximal\nallowed ratio of the longest and shortest side of a rectangle (the\nvalue of this upper bound is set in the constructor of the fair\nsplitting rule). Among the splits that satisfy this bound, it selects\nthe one one in which the points have the largest spread.  It then\nconsiders the most extreme cuts that would be allowed by the aspect\nratio bound. This is done by dividing the longest side of the\nrectangle by the aspect ratio bound. If the median cut lies between\nthese extreme cuts, then we use the median cut. If not, then consider\nthe extreme cut that is closer to the median.  If all the points lie\nto one side of this cut, then we slide the cut until it hits the first\npoint.  This may violate the aspect ratio bound, but will never\ngenerate empty cells.\n<P>\n\n<I>#include &lt;<A HREF=\"../../../include/CGAL/Splitters.h\">CGAL/Splitters.h</A>&gt;</I>\n<P>\n\n<H3>Parameters</H3>\n<P>\n\nExpects for the first template argument a model of\nthe concept <I><A HREF=\"Concept_SearchTraits.html#Cross_link_anchor_1316\">SearchTraits</A></I>, \nfor example <I>CGAL::Cartesian_d&lt;double&gt;</I>.\n<P>\n\nExpects for the second template argument a model of the concept <I>Separator</I>.
