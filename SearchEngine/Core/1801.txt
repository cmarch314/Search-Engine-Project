In the interim, students interested in focusing on statistics are encouraged to consider a minor in Statistics along with a major in a field of interest. </p>\n<p> <cite> &#8250; <a href=\"degree_stats.php#uci\">Is the Statistics minor for me?</a><br/>\n\t\t\t    &#8250; <a href=\"degree_stats.php#get\">What do I take?</a><br/>\n\t\t\t    &#8250; <a href=\"degree_stats.php#what\">What can I do with this minor?</a><br/>\n\t\t      <a href=\"degree_stats.php#visit\"></a></cite> </p>\n<p> <a name=\"uci\"></a> </p>\n<h4>Is the Statistics minor for me? </h4>\n<p>Statistics is  the field associated with the collection, analysis, and interpretation of data.  Statistical theory and methods are relevant in a wide range of fields in the social, physical, and biological sciences, as well as in a range of practical areas like public policy, education, engineering and medicine.   The minor in Statistics is designed to provide students with exposure to both statistical theory and practice.</p>\n<p>The minor requires a total of seven courses. These include a mathematics course, five core statistics courses, and an elective that may be taken from among several departments.  Some of the courses used to complete the minor may include prerequisites that may or may not be part of a student's course requirements for their major. Because of this the minor is somewhat intensive, but it is a useful complement to a variety of undergraduate fields for mathematically inclined students.</p>\n<p>The minor, supplemented with a few additional courses (mathematics and computing), would provide sufficient background for graduate study in statistics. Students considering a minor in Statistics should meet with the Director of Undergraduate Studies in Statistics as early as possible to plan their course work.</p>\n<p><i><strong>NOTE: Students may not receive both a minor in Statistics and a specialization in Statistics within the Mathematics major.</strong></i></p>\n<p><a href=\"degree_stats.php#startcontent\">&#8593; Back to top</a></p>\n<a name=\"get\"></a>\n<h4>What do I take?</h4>\n<p>See the <a href=\"http://catalogue.uci.edu/donaldbrenschoolofinformationandcomputersciences/departmentofstatistics/\">General Catalogue</a> for a full listing of the Statistics minor requirements.</p>\n<a href=\"degree_stats.php#startcontent\">&#8593; Back to top</a><br/>\n<p>\n                <a name=\"what\"></a> </p>\n<h4>What can I do with this minor? </h4>\n<p><img alt=\"photo: student doing an equation on a whiteboard\" class=\"left\" height=\"240\" src=\"../../bin/img/photos/degrees/stats_do.jpg\" width=\"160\"/>The Statistics minor will add a statistical perspective to a student's major field of study.  The statistical perspective emphasizes the important role that uncertainty and variation play in understanding the world around us.   </p>\n<p>A student's  enhanced quantitative reasoning skills will be useful in any future career path -- this includes study towards a professional degree (e.g., law or medicine), graduate study in  a field of interest, or a position in industry or government. </p>\n<p><strong>Students who complete the minor requirements will be able to:<br/>\n              </strong>\n\t\t\t  \n\t\t\t  </p>\n<ul>\n<li>recognize the important role of uncertainty and variability in\n                empirical studies of real world phenomenon</li>\n<li>employ basic statistical methods to analyze and explore data</li>\n<li>understand the theoretical underpinnings of basic statistical methods</li>\n</ul>\n<p>&#8593;<a href=\"degree_stats.php#startcontent\">Back to top</a></p>\n<a name=\"visit\"></a>\n\n\n</div>\n</blockquote>\n</div>\n<div id=\"sidebar\"><div id=\"sidebar_title\">More Undergraduate &#187;</div>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"../sao/index.php\">Academic advising</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../courses/index.php\">Academic year plan</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"index.php\">ICS majors</a>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"Double_Majors.php\">Double major</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"Second_BA.php\">2nd baccalaureate</a></li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n<li><a href=\"Minors.php\">ICS minors</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../QA_Petitions.php\">Petitions</a></li>\n<li><span style=\"font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;
