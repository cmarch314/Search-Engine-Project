It is the <em>student's</em> responsibility to be aware of deadlines and make the modifications to his or her schedule before those deadlines pass.</p>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<!-- br-->\n<hr style=\"width: 100%;\" width=\"100%\"/>\n<!-- br-->\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187;</strong></span> Add, Drop, and Change Deadlines</strong></p>\n<p>Always check the course syllabus carefully for course specific\nadd and drop information. If earlier add/drop deadliness are required by\n the instructor, enrollment will be controlled by the instructor with \nauthorization codes.</p>\n<p>The deadline to add or drop courses and to change grade option or variable units is <em>5:00pm on Friday of Week 2</em>.</p>\n<p>Once these deadlines have passed, an <a href=\"http://www.reg.uci.edu/enrollment/adc/exceptions.html\" target=\"_blank\" title=\"Registrar's Enrollment Exceptions Page\">Enrollment Exception</a> request must be submitted through <a href=\"http://www.reg.uci.edu/access/student/welcome/\" target=\"_blank\" title=\"Student Access Log-In\">Student Access</a>.</p>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<table align=\"center\" border=\"1\" cellpadding=\"3\" cellspacing=\"2\" style=\"border-color: #66ccff;\" summary=\"Timeline of Enrollment Changes\">\n<caption>\n</caption>\n<tbody>\n<tr align=\"center\" style=\"background-color: #ffffcc;\">\n<td colspan=\"3\"><strong>Timeline of Enrollment Changes</strong></td>\n</tr>\n<tr align=\"center\" style=\"background-color: #ffffcc;\">\n<td><strong>Week</strong></td>\n<td><strong>Enrollment Actions</strong></td>\n<td><strong>Notes</strong></td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>1</td>\n<td>Use WebReg to add, drop, and change grade option or variable units.</td>\n<td>No fee applies.</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>2</td>\n<td>Use WebReg to add, drop, and change grade option or variable units.</td>\n<td>The deadline to add, drop or to change grade option or variable units is 5:00pm on Friday.</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>3-6</td>\n<td>An enrollment exception request must be submitted for adds, drops and changes to grade option or variable units. Supporting documentation is required for all exception requests.</td>\n<td>A $3 transaction fee will apply if the request is approved.</td>\n</tr>\n<tr>\n<td>7-10</td>\n<td>An enrollment exception request must be submitted for adds, drops and changes to grade option or variable units. The \"W\" notation on transcript is applied for dropped courses.</td>\n</tr>\n</tbody>\n</table>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187;</strong></span> Submitting Enrollment Exception Requests</strong></p>\n<p>Requests\nto add or drop are reviewed by the academic advising office of the school\noffering the course, as well as the school of the student's major (if different). Requests\nto change grade option or variable units require the approval of the academic\nadvising office of the student's major.</p>\n<p>Enrollment exceptions for adds, drops, and change of grade option or variable units in ICS <span style=\"text-decoration: underline;\">are not guaranteed</span>. Exception requests will only be approved for <em>extenuating and documented circumstances outside of the student's control</em>. It is therefore important to continue attending class meetings and keep up with the assignments until your request is fully processed.</p>\n<p>Requests will be denied if submitted for the following reasons:</p>\n<ul>\n</ul>\n<ul>\n<li>You did notknow the deadlines</li>\n<li>You did not understand the Add/Drop process</li>\n<li>You did not know the grade requirements for your degree</li>\n<li>The course is not required to meet Major, School, or University requirements</li>\n<li>You are doing poorly in the course because of difficulties with the course material</li>\n<li>You are doing poorly in the course because of a heavy course/work load</li>\n<li>You are doing poorly in the course because of failure to attend</li>\n</ul>\n<p>If your drop request is approved, please\nlet your professor know as a courtesy once you have formally dropped the course.</p>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187; </strong></span>Course Conflicts</strong></p>\n<p>Time conflicts between courses may be approved on a case-by-case basis. Note that <em>a lecture to lecture conflict <strong>will not</strong> be approved under <strong>any</strong> circumstances.</em> If a course's lab or discussion time conflicts with another course's lecture, lab, or discussion, AND there are open spaces in both conflicting sections, follow these steps:</p>\n<ul>\n</ul>\n<ul>\n</ul>\n<ol>\n<li>Enroll in one of the conflicting courses (if the conflict involves a lecture, be sure to enroll in the lecture).</li>\n<li>Obtain written approval from both instructors to allow the conflict.</li>\n<li>Forward that approval to <a href=\"mailto:ucounsel@uci.edu\">ucounsel@uci.edu</a>. Be sure to provide the 5-digit course codes for the conflicting courses.</li>\n<li>Allow ICS Student Affairs 2-3 business days to process the request.
