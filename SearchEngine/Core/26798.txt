She has been with The Boeing Company for \nmore than 14 years. During her management of the software development \nfor the Evolved Expendable Launch Vehicle/Delta IV Program, two \nSoftware Development Capability Evaluations (SDCEs) were conducted. \nKaren has also been the Software Engineering Process Group Lead for \nBoeing/McDonnell Douglas Aerospace, Space and Defense Systems and \nTransport Aircraft for five years from 1991 through 1996. Under her \nleadership Boeing conducted four successful SPAs, one CBA-IPI, and \nparticipated in Software Capability Evaluation (SCE). She has a \nBachelor's degree in Mathematics from California State University. \nKaren has over 22 years experience, ranging from commercial to \naerospace software development and major software subcontract \nmanagement, including employment at Hughes, Honeywell and Ford Aerospace.\n\n<p>\n\n<strong>Biography: </strong>\nMajor Christine K. Bonniksen has been the Evolved Expendable \nLaunch Vehicle (EELV) Senior Program Manager for the last three years \noverseeing all EELV work performed by The Boeing Company. During this \ntime the government performed two Software Development Capability \nEvaluations (SDCE) on the EELV program for which she performed the \ngovernment's management function.
