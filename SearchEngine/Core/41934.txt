comparisions of either OEMolBases or\nOEMCMolBases as either the reference or fit molecule.  For the OEMolBase vs\nOEMolBase comparison, the RMSD is the return value.  For the other two cases,\nthe RMSDs are returned in the <code>rmsds</code> array.  The <code>rmsds</code> array\npassed to this function should be of length <code>fit.GetMaxConfIdx()</code> for the\nOEMolBase vs OEMCMolBase overload and\n<code>ref.GetMaxConfIdx()*fit.GetMaxConfIdx()</code> for the OEMCMolBase vs\nOEMCMolBase overload.  The automorph flag indicates whether automorphisms\nshould be taken into account during the RMSD calculation.  Automorphisms are\nthe symmetry related transformations of a molecule which can result in\nanamolously high RMSDs if not properly treated.  For instance, t-butyl-benzene\nhas a three-fold automorphism around the t-butyl group and a two-fold\nautomorphism around the benzene ring.  The heavyOnly flag indicates whether\nonly heavy atoms should be considered or hydrogen atoms should be considered as\nwell.  It is strongly recommended that one should consider carefully before\nsetting the <code>automorph</code> flag to true and the <code>heavyOnly</code> flag to\nfalse.
