   \n<center><H1>Southern California Software Process Improvement Network(SPIN)</H2>\n<H2> Charter </H2>  </center>                      \n\n<H4>I.   ESTABLISHMENT</H4>\n\nThe Southern California Software Process Improvement Network, hereafter \nreferred to as the Southern California SPIN, was originally established on 24\nJanuary 1992 as an activity sponsored by the University of California's Irvine\nResearch Unit in Software (IRUS).\n\n<h4>II.  MISSION </h4>\n\n     The Southern California SPIN is a leadership forum for the free and \nopen exchange of software process improvement experiences and practical\nideas.  We promote achieving higher levels of process maturity, software \nquality and mutual respect.  Our goal is to enhance skills and support \ntechnology transition through an active program of networking, publications,\nrecognition of excellence, and mutual support.\n\n<h4>III. OBJECTIVES</h4>\n\nIn carrying out its mission, the Southern California SPIN organization \nacts to accomplish the following objectives within the software community:\n\n<ul> \n<li>Advance the quality of software;\n\n<li>Improve software productivity;\n\n<li>Promote software process maturity;\n\n<li>Transfer software improvement knowledge and innovation to\n    all participants;\n\n<li> Share lessons learned between participants and other SPIN organizations;\n\n<li> Promote cooperation between industry, academia, and government with \n     respect to software process improvement technology.\n</ul>\n\n<h4>IV.  ORGANIZATION</h4>\n\nThe Southern California SPIN is organized as a Steering\nCommittee, various working groups, general participants, and individual \nmeeting coordinators.  Steering Committee members, working group\nmembers, general participants and meeting coordinators receive no \nremuneration for their services.\n\nThe Southern California SPIN is sponsored by the Irvine Research Unit in \nSoftware (IRUS), an organization chartered by the University of California, \nIrvine. SPIN roles and responsibilities are shared by members of the SPIN \nSteering Committee and IRUS staff.\n\n<h4>A.   STEERING COMMITTEE</h4>\n\nThe Steering Committee consists of those SPIN participants who have \ncommitted to actively take on the roles and responsibilities of the various \nfunctions necessary to run the Southern California SPIN. The Steering \nCommittee operates as a consensus based decision making team with the following\nspecific roles:  leader, deputy leader, IRUS representative, recorder, \nliaison, working group chairs and members-at-large.  One or more individuals \nmay fulfill a specific role. This is not a hierarchically organized team; all \nroles have equal voice in the decision making process.\n\nThe Steering Committee meets after the general SPIN meeting.  The\nmeetings are open to all interested SPIN participants.  Generally these \nmeetings occur over lunch.  \n\nThe responsibilities of the Steering Committee are as follows:\n\n<ul>\n<li>Manage the affairs of the Southern California SPIN;</ut>\n\n<li>In coordination with IRUS, generate the operating budget. This budget \nshould cover all SPIN- related costs except personnel time;\n\n<li>The budget must be approved by the IRUS Executive Committee to ensure \n     compliance with University of California Research Unit regulations.\n\n<li>The SPIN budget funds may not be appropriated if the IRUS budget \nexperiences a shortfall.\n\n<li>Maintain coordination with other SPIN organizations;\n\n<li>Identify program coordinators for the SPIN meetings;\n\n<li>Approve topics and speakers for SPIN meetings;\n\n<li>Approve the establishment, charter, and disbandment of working groups, \n    and selection of working group chairs;\n\n<li>Elect Steering Committee members to the roles described in \n    this charter;\n\n<li>Periodically review the operation of the SPIN to determine\n    improvements in its operation. This includes reviewing the charter \n    at least annually and reviewing the budget at least bi-annually.</ul>\n\n<p>1. <i>Responsibilities of Steering Committee Leader</i>\n\n<p>The leader presides at regular meetings of the Southern\nCalifornia SPIN and at meetings of the Steering Committee. The leader \nis responsible for assuring the reassignment of duties of other\nSteering Committee members in the event of their absence, or in\nthe event of incapacity, until they are replaced by the Steering Committee.  \nThe leader maintains a list of current role assignments. \n\n<p> The leader must be a member of an IRUS member organization.  The leader \n    ensures that IRUS interests are maintained.\n\n<p>2.<i> Responsibilities of the SPIN Steering Committee Deputy Leader</i>\n\n<p>The Deputy Leader takes over the leader's duties when the leader\n   is not available, and such duties as the leader may delegate.\n\n<p>3.<i> Responsibilities of IRUS Representative</i>\n\n<p> The IRUS representative is the custodian of IRUS funds.  The IRUS\nrepresentative renders an annual report of the SPIN budget within \nthirty (30) days of the end of the IRUS fiscal year and six months\nthereafter, plus any special reports required by the Steering\nCommittee.  The IRUS representative monitors all expenditures against \nthe budget and ensures that these expenditures are in compliance\nwith university policies, regulations and procedures.\n\n<p>The IRUS representative is the liaison with the IRUS Executive Committee.\n\n<p>The IRUS representative carries out the desires of the SPIN\nSteering Committee in accordance with the SPIN operating budget.\n\n<p>The IRUS representative is responsible for securing the meeting\nsite and arrangement of refreshments for the SPIN and SPIN Steering \nCommittee meetings.\n\n<p>The IRUS representative is responsible for producing the meeting flyer.\n\n<p>The IRUS representative is responsible for maintaining a database\nof the current SPIN participants.\n\n<p>The IRUS representative is responsible for maintaining all literature\npertinent to the mission of the organization, including all Steering Committee\nand working group meeting minutes and any available presentation materials.\n\n<p>The IRUS representative takes over the responsibilities of the\ndeputy leader in the event of an absence until the deputy leader either \nreturns or is replaced by the Steering Committee.  \n\n<p>4. <i>Responsibilities of Recorder</i>\n<p>The recorder is responsible for recording and disseminating Steering \nCommittee meeting minutes. The recorder conveys these minutes to the IRUS \nrepresentative.\n\n<p> 5. <i>Responsibilities of Liaison</i>\n\n<p>The liaison is the point of contact for other SPINs, professional\norganizations (e.g., IEEE, ACM), and other organizations such as the SEI.  \nThe liaison coordinates with these organizations to transfer information \nand disseminate information to Steering Committee members.  \n\n<p>The liaison distributes related information that  the Steering\nCommittee feels is of general interest to the current SPIN participants.\n\n<p>6. <i>Responsibilities of Working Group Chairs</i>\n\n<p>Working groups are established by the Steering Committee for the conduct of \nspecific, special interest activities.  The chair is approved by the Steering \nCommittee. The chair of each working group is responsible for approving all \nmembers of the working group and for ensuring that meeting minutes are \nforwarded to the IRUS representative.  The chair disseminates the minutes to \nall working group members.  The working group is responsible for developing a \ncharter that is approved by the Steering Committee. The chair or designee \nis required to give periodic status reports to the Steering Committee.\n\n<p>7. <i>Responsibilities of Members-At-Large</i>\n\n<p>Any SPIN participant may petition to the Steering Committee to become a \nmember-at-large.  The appointment must be approved by the Steering Committee. \nMembers-at-large regularly attend and fully participate in Steering Committee \nmeetings.\n\n<h4>B.   GENERAL PARTICIPANTS</h4>\n\n<p>Participation in the Southern California SPIN is open without discrimination\nto all individuals, companies, academic institutions, and government \norganizations who are interested in furthering the objectives of the \nSouthern California SPIN.\n\nMembership in the Irvine Research Unit in Software is encouraged,\nthough not required.  Participation in SPIN does not imply membership in IRUS.\n\n<h4>C.   MEETING COORDINATOR</h4>\n\n<p>A meeting coordinator is chosen for each meeting by the Steering Committee. \nThe meeting coordinator is responsible for arrangement of the meeting and for\nproviding complete flyer information to the IRUS representative at least \none month in advance.  The meeting coordinator is responsible for reviewing \nthe meeting planning activities with the Steering Committee and for ensuring \nthe quality of the meeting.  The meeting coordinator introduces each speaker \nand acts as a time keeper to ensure that the meeting runs according to \nschedule.  The meeting coordinator ensures that adequate copies of the \npresentation material are available at the time of the SPIN meeting.\n\n<h4>V.   TERMS OF ROLE ASSIGNMENTS</h4>\n\nRole assignments must be reviewed and elected by a majority vote at least \non an annual basis coinciding with the Southern California SPIN calendar year.\n\n<p>\nAny appointment can be rescinded by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the \nSteering Committee at a meeting where a majority of the Steering Committee \nmembers are present.\n\n<h4>VI.  PARTICIPANT FEES</h4>\n\nAny fees for meetings or special events are determined by the Steering \nCommittee and IRUS.<p>\n\nAnnual IRUS membership dues are handled by the IRUS representative. <p>\n\n<h4>VII. SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA SPIN CALENDAR YEAR</h4>\n\nThe Southern California SPIN calendar year will run from July 1 to June 30.\n\n<h4>VIII.     MEETINGS</h4>\n\nMeetings of the Southern California SPIN will normally take place on the last \nFriday of each month at the campus of the University of California, Irvine.  \nAn announcement for each meeting will be disseminated to all current \nparticipants at least two weeks in advance of the meeting.\n\n<h4>IX.  CHARTER REVISIONS</h4>\n\nProposed charter revisions are distributed and announced in advance of a \ngeneral SPIN meeting. Charter revisions must be approved at a subsequent \nSteering Committee meeting, where the approval has been identified as an \nagenda item, by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the participants in the\nvote.  At least fifty (50) percent of the Southern California SPIN Steering \nCommittee must participate in the vote for the vote to be valid.\n\n<h4>X.   DISSOLUTION OF ORGANIZATION</h4>\n\nDissolution of the Southern California SPIN organization shall consist of \nunanimous agreement of all Steering Committee members, together with a \nmajority vote at a meeting that has been publicized in advance to all current \nparticipants of the Southern California SPIN for the purpose of taking this \nvote.<p>\n\n<P>\n<HR>\n<ADDRESS>\nIrvine Research Unit in Software<BR>\n<A HREF=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/\">\nDepartment of Information and Computer Science</A><BR>\nUniversity of California, Irvine  CA 92697-3425\n</ADDRESS><br>\nThis charter was last revised on 05 December 1996.\n</BODY></HTML>\n\n", "id": 26717.0}
