\t\t\t\tEvolving nature of books: 1    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>Books have played a dominant role in the evolution of human cuture.\u00c2\u00a0 Interestingly, technology played equally important role in the evolution of books.\u00c2\u00a0 Starting from the days of papyrus to today, technology shaped books.\u00c2\u00a0 Now the technology has brought us to a very interesting pint that is chalenging the nature of books from many perspectives and this is likely to result in disruptive changes in books.</p>\n<p>A minor change is coming because of the nature of media.\u00c2\u00a0 Books were developed under an implied assumption that the medium used to store, archive, and communicate knowledge is text.\u00c2\u00a0 Photos found their way in books as technology evolved.\u00c2\u00a0 But in the traditional form of books audio and video could not find a place because of their continuous media (temporal) nature.\u00c2\u00a0 Now that technology has made it easier to capture, archive, present, and communicate information and knowledge in the form of audio, photos, and video the text dominant nature of books as the source of knowledge is strongly challenged.</p>\n<p>Another change that is chalenging the nature of books is that traditional story teling was a predominantly sequential process.\u00c2\u00a0 Though books did allow some nonlinearity in reading, but authors assumed that most people will read it in the sequence they presented the content or will follow their suggestions about the sequence.\u00c2\u00a0 With Internet culture came the culture of &#8216;nonlinearity&#8217; &#8212; now readers are used to follow their own instincts lot more than author&#8217;s suggestion.\u00c2\u00a0 They have started jumping left and right, up and down, and forward and backward.\u00c2\u00a0 They jump every way they can to make use of a book &#8212; particularly when it is available in electronic form.\u00c2\u00a0 Moreover, they are now getting used to performing different types of surgery on these books &#8212; also called cut-and-paste &#8212; to create the book that they consider more meaningful than what one or more authors presented.</p>\n<p>One more factor that is challenging the nature of books is their bounded nature.\u00c2\u00a0 A book was of a finite size or length when it was finished.\u00c2\u00a0 This is being chalenged now.\u00c2\u00a0 Once the book does not have to be on a paper and be a physically bound volume, there is no reason to have a hard finish to it.\u00c2\u00a0 You may want to finish it and call it a finished book or you could keep it growing.\u00c2\u00a0 As new material and new thoughts come, the book keeps growing.\u00c2\u00a0 In a sense, the book is organic.\u00c2\u00a0 It has a state at any given time but the state keeps evolving based on what direction the author wants to take it.</p>\n<p>But wait, the way things are becoming popular, there could be many authors of a book.\u00c2\u00a0 And these authors don&#8217;t have to have any preagreed roles and responsibilities.\u00c2\u00a0 Anyone could join in a book project that somebody else started.\u00c2\u00a0 Everybody starts contributing what they consider is important.\u00c2\u00a0 There is only loose control on what gets contributed by whom and what direction the project really evolves.\u00c2\u00a0 We have seen this happen in case of Wikis.\u00c2\u00a0 But now Wikis are going to become multimedia so any person can contribute what (s)he considers to be the appropriate media content to theis organic book.\u00c2\u00a0</p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta-bar clearfix\">\t        \t\t\t\r\n    \t\t\t<div
