\t\t\t\tJim Gray    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>Jim Gray has been a very respected name not only in databases but in whole computer science.\u00c2\u00a0 In addition to his technical competence, he has always been a very enthusiastic researcher eager to address challenging problem and a very friendly person.\u00c2\u00a0 No wonder people are so concerned and are making every effort to locate him.\u00c2\u00a0 He went on his sail boat last Sunday to spread ashes of his mother in ocean and has not been located since then.</p>\n<p>NYT has a very detailed and touching <a title=\"Gray\" href=\"http://www.nytimes.com/2007/02/03/technology/03search.html?_r=1&#038;th&#038;emc=th&#038;oref=slogin\">story detailing search</a> for Jim Gray using all modern technology.\u00c2\u00a0</p>\n<blockquote><p><!--StartFragment --></p>\n<p>The flurry of activity, which began in earnest on Tuesday, escalated as the days and nights passed. A veritable Who\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Who of computer scientists from <a title=\"Google\" href=\"http://www.nytimes.com/mem/MWredirect.html?MW=http://custom.marketwatch.com/custom/nyt-com/html-companyprofile.asp&#038;symb=GOOG\">Google</a>, <a title=\"Amazon\" href=\"http://www.nytimes.com/mem/MWredirect.html?MW=http://custom.marketwatch.com/custom/nyt-com/html-companyprofile.asp&#038;symb=AMZN\">Amazon</a>, Microsoft, <a title=\"More articles about the National Aeronautics and Space Administration.\" href=\"http://topics.nytimes.com/top/reference/timestopics/organizations/n/national_aeronautics_and_space_administration/index.html?inline=nyt-org\">NASA</a> and universities across the country spent sleepless nights writing ad hoc software, creating a blog and reconfiguring satellite images so that dozens of volunteers could pore over them, searching for a speck of red hull and white deck among a sea of gray pixels.</p>\n<p>Coast Guard officials said they had never before seen such a concerted, technically creative effort carried out by friends and family of a missing sailor. \u00e2\u20ac\u0153This is the largest strictly civilian, privately sponsored search effort I have ever seen,\u00e2\u20ac? said Capt. David Swatland, deputy commander of the Coast Guard sector in San Francisco, who has spent most of his 23-year career in search and rescue.</p></blockquote>\n<p>Lets just hope that something strange, but not bad, happened and Jim will come back safe to make his family and friends happy and to help advance computer science and its applications further.</p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div
