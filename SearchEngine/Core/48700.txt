\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p>It was going to happen &#8212; but did not anticipate that it will happen so soon.</p>\n<p>Cell phones will use GPS to bridge the cyberspace with the physical world &#8212; <a href=\"http://www.nytimes.com/2006/06/28/technology/28locate.html?pagewanted=1&#038;_r=2&#038;th&#038;emc=th\">a nice story in NYT today</a>.</p>\n<blockquote><p>If you stand on a street corner in Tokyo today you can point a specialized cellphone at a hotel, a restaurant or a historical monument, and with the press of a button the phone will display information from the Internet describing the object you are looking at.</p>\n<p>The new service is made possible by the efforts of three Japanese companies and GeoVector, a small American technology firm, and it represents a missing link between cyberspace and the physical world. </p>\n<p>The phones combine satellite-based navigation, precise to within 30 feet or less, with an electronic compass to provide a new dimension of orientation. Connect the device to the Internet and it is possible to overlay the point-and-click simplicity of a computer screen on top of the real world.</p></blockquote>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div
