Nef polyhedron is a subset of the 3-dimensional space that is the\nresult of forming complements and intersections starting from a finite\nset <I>H</I> of 3-dimensional halfspaces. Nef polyhedra are closed\nunder all binary set operations, i.e., <A HREF=\"../Kernel_23_ref/Function_intersection.html#Cross_link_anchor_221\">intersection</A>, union,\ndifference, complement, and under the topological operations boundary,\nclosure, and interior.\n<P>\n\nA 3D Nef polyhedron can be represented by the local pyramids of the minimal\nelements of its incidence structure. Without going into to much detail, a local\npyramid essentially reflects the topologic and geometric situation at a certain\nlocation in a point set. For finite polyhedra the minimal elements \nof the incidence structure are vertices only. This means, that it suffices to \nmodel the topological and geometric situation of the vertices. For \n3D Nef polyhedra, the local pyramid of a vertex is represented by \na planar Nef polyhedra embedded on a sphere.\n<P>\n\nA <I><A HREF=\"Class_Nef_polyhedron_3.html#Cross_link_anchor_747\">Nef_polyhedron_3</A></I> consists of vertices <I>V</I>, a sphere map for each\nvertex in <I>V</I>, edges <I>E</I>, facets <I>F</I>, volumes <I>C</I>, a mark\nfor every item, and an incidence relation on them. Each edge and each facet\nis represented by two halfedges or two halffacets, respectively.\n<P>\n\n<I>#include &lt;<A
