class=\"single-entry-content\">\r\n\t\t<p>Welcome to CRADL Lab at the Donald Bren School of Information &amp; Computer Sciences at the University of California, Irvine!</p>\n<div id=\"attachment_639\" style=\"width: 410px\" class=\"wp-caption alignright\"><a href=\"http://cradl.ics.uci.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/Group-Shot.jpg\"><img class=\"size-medium wp-image-639\" alt=\"Group-Shot\" src=\"http://cradl.ics.uci.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/Group-Shot-400x265.jpg\" width=\"400\" height=\"265\" /></a><p class=\"wp-caption-text\">CRADL group in April 2014. Pictured from left to right: Marco Aurelio Gerosa, Oliver Yi Wang, David Redmiles, Hiroko Wilensky, Benjamin Koehne, Ban AlAni. Not pictured: Werner Beuschel and Matthew Bietz</p></div>\n<p>CRADL is a research group led by <a title=\"David Redmiles, Professor\" href=\"http://cradl.ics.uci.edu/?page_id=104\">Prof. David Redmiles</a> that employs an interdisciplinary approach to research phenomena in human collaborative activity. We primarily study collaborative work, and, particularly, software engineering.</p>\n<p>The acronym CRADL stands for Collaboration Research in Action, Design, and Learning. Our research focus is on <em>collaboration</em>. The themes of <em>action</em>, <em>design</em>, and <em>learning</em> have various inspirations and meanings. In a simplistic sense, people act in the world. In so doing, they cause or achieve changes and designs. Actions do not always succeed and designs often fail. Reflection leads to learning. Our work is inspired by, among others, Schoen&#8217;s concept of reflection in action, the centrality of activity in the work by activity theorists such as Engestrom, Kuuti, et al., the science of design work such as by Simon and Fischer, and intelligent tutoring research by Anderson.</p>\n<p>If you have any questions or comments, please contact us at <a title=\"CRADL Contact\"
