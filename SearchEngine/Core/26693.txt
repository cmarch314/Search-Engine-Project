(revised)<br>\nJuly 25, 1997 (accepted)<br> \nJuly 31, 1998 (revised and accepted)<br> </center>\n\n<p><br><p><br>\n\n\n<strong>I.<i>  Scope</strong></i>\n\n<p>\n\nThis procedure specifies the requirements for conducting the annual election \nfor Southern California Software Process Improvement Network (SPIN) Steering \nCommittee roles. It is considered a supplement to the Southern California SPIN \nCharter and does not override or supersede any item(s) specified in the \ncharter.\n\n<P>\n\n<strong>II.<i>  Term</strong></i>\n<p>\n\nThe term for all Steering Committee roles is October 1 of the\nyear in which the vote is held through September 30 of the following year.\n\n<p>\n<strong>III.<i>  Nominations</strong></i>\n\n<P>\n\nAny member of the Steering Committee may nominate any Steering Committee \nmember, including him/herself. Nominations are made to either the Steering \nCommittee Leader or the IRUS Representative. Nominations must be completed no \nlater than four weeks prior to the August Steering Committee meeting.\n\n<P>\n\nMultiple candidates may be nominated to jointly fill a role.\n\n<P>\n\nCandidates must accept or refuse a nomination no later than three weeks prior \nto the August Steering Committee meeting. Acceptance/refusal notifications \nare made to the Steering Committee Leader or the IRUS Representative.\n\n<P>\n\n<strong>IV.<i>  Position Statements</strong></i>\n\n<p>\n\nCandidates for the Steering Committee Leader role should write a position \nstatement and submit it to the IRUS Representative no later than two weeks \nprior to the August Steering Committee meeting. The IRUS Representative \ndistributes the position statements to the Steering Committee members no \nlater than one week prior to the August Steering Committee meeting.\n\n<P>\n\n<strong>V. <i> Voting</strong></i>\n\n<P>\n\nThe vote is held at the August Steering Committee meeting and is conducted by \nsecret ballot supplied by the IRUS Representative. Absentee ballots of \nqualified voters are accepted by the IRUS Representative until the close-of-\nbusiness the day before the August meeting. An absentee ballot becomes \ninvalid if the voter is present to vote in person at the August Steering \nCommittee meeting.\n\n<P>\n\n<strong>VI.<i>  Qualification of Voter</strong></i>\n\n<P>\n\nSteering Committee membership is reviewed by the Steering Committee at the \nJuly meeting. Voting is limited to members with regular attendance as \ndetermined by attendance of at least three (3) of the six (6) meetings \nimmediately preceding the vote.\n\n<P>\n\n<strong>VII. <i> Ballot Count</strong></i>\n\n<P>\n\nBallots are counted by the IRUS Representative and a volunteer who is not a \ncandidate for a role. Both count the ballots separately and concur on the \ncount. A simple majority decides the winner. A coin toss breaks a tie.\n\n<P>\n\n<strong>VIII.<i> Change of Voting Date</strong></i>\n\n<p>\n\nIf the vote is not held in August, it is instead conducted at the September \nSteering Committee meeting.\n\n<P>\n\n<strong>IX.<i>  Incomplete Terms</strong></i>\n\n<P>\n\nIn the event that the Steering Committee Leader is unable to complete his/her \nterm, the Deputy Leader acts as Steering Committee Leader for the duration of \nthe current term.\n\n<P>\n\nIf the holder of another role is unable to complete the term, the Steering \nCommittee Leader requests a volunteer for that role. If more than one person \nvolunteers for the role, a vote is held at the next Steering Committee \nmeeting. \n\n<P>\n\nPersons fulfilling incomplete terms carry out the duties of that role for the \nduration of the current term.\n\n<p>\n<A HREF=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/IRUS\">Back to the IRUS home page</A>\n\n<HR>\n<ADDRESS>\n<A HREF=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/IRUS/\">\nIrvine Research Unit in Software</A><BR>\n<A HREF=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/\">\nInformation and Computer Science</A><BR>\nUniversity of California, Irvine  CA 92697-3425 <BR>\n</ADDRESS>\n</body>\n</html>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n", "id": 26693.0}
