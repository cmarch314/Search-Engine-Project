are here to help students work towards those goals,\nand academic advising is an intentional partnership between the student and\nhis/her advisor(s) in which the shared goal is the student&#8217;s academic\nsuccess. </p>\n<p>Below is information on when, why, and how often to see academic advising, whether you come during <a href=\"Appts_Walk-Ins.php\" target=\"_blank\" title=\"Appointments and Walk-in Comparison\">walk-in hours or for an appointment</a>.</p>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<hr style=\"width: 100%;\" width=\"100%\"/>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187;</strong></span> Meet with an Academic Advisor<br/></strong></p>\n<ul>\n<li>Whenever you have a question about your academic standing, progress, or goals</li>\n<li>Once a quarter, or at least once a year, to make sure you are on track</li>\n<li>Three quarters <em>before</em> your intended graduation date</li>\n</ul>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187;</strong></span> Some Benefits of Consistent Academic Advising Meetings<br/></strong></p>\n<ul>\n<li>Be confident that you are on track for graduation</li>\n<li>Receive assistance with short- and long-term goal setting</li>\n<li>Get help with planning and selecting courses to meet your academic and career goals</li>\n<li>Obtain referrals to and advice about campus resources and\nopportunities</li>\n<li>Gain clarification on University and School policies and procedures</li>\n</ul>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187;</strong></span> While Meeting with an Advisor<br/></strong></p>\n<ul>\n<li>Arrive on time (if meeting during a scheduled appointment)</li>\n<li>Come prepared with questions and concerns</li>\n<li>Honestly discuss your goals, interests, and priorities</li>\n<li>Keep an open mind to consider the options presented to you</li>\n<li>Don&#8217;t be afraid to ask questions if you don't understand something</li>\n</ul>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187;</strong></span> What it is a Study Plan, and Why Should You Have One?</strong></p>\n<p>A Study Plan maps out of the courses and/or units you would need\nto take in the upcoming academic year(s) in order to make progress on your\ndegree requirements.</p>\n<ul>\n<li>It presents a bird's eye view of your course load and potential\nacademic commitments over the year</li>\n<li>It allows you to make quick adjustments to your schedule and plan ahead</li>\n<li>It can help reduce stress when enrolling for classes </li>\n</ul>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #6699cc;\"><strong>&#187;</strong></span> Tips for Building a Study Plan</strong></p>\n<ul>\n<li>Know what classes you need to take. These include courses that\nsatisfy University and School Requirements, as well as any core courses\nrequired for your major.</li>\n<li>Make special note of any required classes that are only offered once\na year or once every other year, or must be taken in a specific order.</li>\n<li>Pay attention to course prerequisites, and know which ones you have\ncleared. If you need to submit a prerequisite clearing request, take care of it\nin advance. </li>\n<li>Use the tentative academic year course plan when mapping out your\nyear. But keep in mind that this course plan is tentative, so some of the\nelective classes you were hoping to take may be cancelled or conflict with each\nother. </li>\n<li>Be prepared to be flexible!</li>\n</ul>\n\n\n</div>\n</blockquote>\n</div>\n<div id=\"sidebar\"><div id=\"sidebar_title\">More Undergraduate &#187;</div>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"index.php\">Academic advising</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../courses/index.php\">Academic year plan</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../degrees/index.php\">ICS majors</a>\n<ul>\n<li><a href=\"../degrees/Double_Majors.php\">Double major</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../degrees/Second_BA.php\">2nd baccalaureate</a></li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n<li><a href=\"../degrees/Minors.php\">ICS minors</a></li>\n<li><a href=\"../QA_Petitions.php\">Petitions</a></li>\n<li><span style=\"font-family: Verdana,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;
