\t\t\t\tPhoto Eco System: Digital cameras    \t\t\t</h1><!-- .entry-title -->\r\n  \t\t\t</header>\r\n\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content clearfix\">\r\n    \t\t\t<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\">A modern digital camera is no longer just a simple pinhole camera (the old camera obscura model) that records only optical information about the environment as a projection\u00c2\u00a0 of intensity values on a chemical surface.\u00c2\u00a0 A modern day digital camera, even the simplest point-and-shoot camera that is now used by close to a billion people, has many different types of \u00c2\u00a0sensors and a flash to capture and modify the environment. Along with the intensity values, it records several additional optical and other parameters. Most importantly, it records the focal length, aperture opening, aperture time, and flash-related information. It also stores the clock and the camera model.<br />\n</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\">In many cameras, a user can set a specific scene mode to declare the intended nature of the image so the camera can adjust appropriate parameters\u00e2\u20ac\u201d and this mode is also recorded with the picture.\u00c2\u00a0<br />\n</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\">\u00c2\u00a0</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\">Already cameras are available with GPS, and this functionality will become common soon, so you\u00e2\u20ac\u2122ll know exactly where the picture was taken. In short, a digital camera is no longer a simple camera; it\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s a multimedia device that stores the environment where a photo is taken. All this information is explicitly available along with the picture\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s intensity values. Most digital cameras use standards like the exchangeable image format<br />\n</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\">(EXIF) that stores all this and more information in each digital photo (see http://www.exif.org for<br />\n</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\">more about EXIF).<br />\n</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\">\u00c2\u00a0</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\">What is equally important to realize and remember is that the intensity values for mega-pixels \u00e2\u20ac\u201c currently 10 Mega-pixel seems to be the target \u00e2\u20ac\u201c are stored in several mega bytes but all other information is stored in almost negligible size of a few Kilo bytes at max. \u00c2\u00a0Also this information, commonly called meta data, is explicitly available while intensity values are a convolution of many factors and must be analyzed rigorously to get any information out of those. \u00c2\u00a0<br />\n</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\">\u00c2\u00a0</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\">Most research papers in image retrieval demonstrate search-related solutions as if we can solve the problem using only intensity values.\u00c2\u00a0 Research in this area has been going on for more than a decade with several thousand researchers addressing this problem in almost all technologically developed and developing countries.\u00c2\u00a0 Most, at least more than 95% research has been to somehow utilize intensity values to sole the problem of indexing, organizing, and retrieving photos.\u00c2\u00a0 I could understand the obsession with using only intensity values some time ago \u00e2\u20ac\u201c when digital cameras were not common \u00e2\u20ac\u201c but now in the changed situation I expected that the problem of photo retrieval will be redefined in the research community. \u00c2\u00a0It\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s a mystery to me that researchers are still reluctant to use all of the other obvious information that\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s easily available and helpful.<br />\n</font><font face=\"Times New Roman\">\u00c2\u00a0</font></p>\n<p><font face=\"Times New Roman\">If we consider all the information available from digital cameras in digital photos, we can get a significant head start, even before we open the so-called image. One important factor to remember is that all this information\u00e2\u20ac\u201dlet\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s call it metadata in digital photos\u00e2\u20ac\u201dis easily available and can be processed several orders of magnitude faster than processing the images themselves (which are becoming increasingly larger in size).<br />\n</font></p>\n  \t\t\t</div>\r\n  \t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta-bar
