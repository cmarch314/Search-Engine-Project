[proficiency]</li>\n</ul>\n\n<strong>General trees</strong>\n\n<ul>\n<li>Recognize and generate preorder, postorder, and level-order \ntraversals.  [proficiency]</li>\n<li>Describe a tree using conventional terms (parent, child, ancestor, \ndescendent, node, leaf, root, height).  [proficiency]</li>\n<li>Describe implementation using linked structures.  [proficiency]</li>\n</ul>\n\n<strong>Binary trees</strong>\n\n<ul>\n<li>Apply basic definitions and terminology about binary trees (e.g., \nparent, descendant, height).  [mastery]</li>\n<li>Determine the pre-, in-, post-, and level-order traversals of a \ngiven binary tree.  [mastery]</li>\n<li>Implement recursive traversal of binary trees and simple \napplications (such as recursive computation of height).  [mastery]</li>\n</ul>\n\n<strong>Priority queues</strong>\n\n<ul>\n<li>Describe the abstract behavior (for example, by describing changes \nor results of a series of operations) of a priority queue, including \nenqueue, dequeue, front/peek, and isempty operations. [proficiency]</li>\n<li>Derive recurrence for average-case behavior of binary search trees. \n [exposure]</li>\n<li>Do insertions and deletions in skip lists on paper.  [proficiency]</li>\n<li>Execute hashing with separate chaining on paper.  [mastery]</li>\n<li>Implement a hash table using separate chaining.  [proficiency]</li>\n<li>Evaluate choices of divisor in division hashing.  [mastery]</li>\n<li>Understand when open addressing is an appropriate technique.  \n[exposure]</li>\n<li>Construct hash functions for strings and other objects.  \n[proficiency]</li>\n</ul>\n\n<strong>Graphs</strong>\n\n<ul>\n<li>Apply definitions and terminology (e.g., directed vs. undirected \ngraphs, cycles, paths, components).  [mastery]</li>\n<li>Understand the characteristics and relative advantages of adjacency \nmatrix vs. adjacency list representations.  [mastery]</li>\n<li>Perform a depth-first search and breadth-first search on paper.  \n[mastery]</li>\n<li>Implement depth-first search and breadth-first search.
