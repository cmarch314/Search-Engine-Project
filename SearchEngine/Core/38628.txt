Interpolation methods based on natural\nneighbor coordinates are particularly interesting because they adapt\neasily to non-uniform and highly anisotropic data.  This package\ncontains Sibson's <MATH><I>C<SUP>1</SUP></I></MATH> continuous interpolation method which\ninterpolates exactly spherical quadrics (of the form <MATH><I><IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=10 HEIGHT=12 ALIGN=BOTTOM SRC=\"cc_Phi.gif\">(<B>x</B>)\n=a + <B>b</B><SUP>t</SUP> <B>x</B> +<IMG BORDER=0 WIDTH=9 HEIGHT=17 ALIGN=MIDDLE SRC=\"cc_gamma.gif\"> <B>x</B><SUP>t</SUP><B>x</B></I></MATH>) and\nFarin's <MATH><I>C<SUP>1</SUP></I></MATH> continuous interpolation method based on\nBernstein-B&eacute;zier techniques and interpolating exactly quadratic\nfunctions - assuming that the function gradient is known. In\naddition, Sibson defines a method to approximate the function\ngradients for data points that are in the interior of the convex hull.\nThis\nmethod is exact for spherical quadrics.\n<P>\n\nThis C<SMALL>GAL</SMALL> package implements Sibson's and Farin's interpolation\nfunctions as well as Sibson's function gradient fitting method.\nFurthermore, it provides functions to compute the natural neighbor\ncoordinates with respect to a two-dimensional Voronoi diagram (i. e. \nfrom the Delaunay triangulation of the data points) and to a\ntwo-dimensional power diagram for weighted points (i. e. from their\nregular triangulation).  Natural neighbor coordinates on closed and\nwell-sampled surfaces can also be computed if the normal to the\nsurface at the query point is known.
