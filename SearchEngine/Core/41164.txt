The basic task of such an operator is to produce a\nrepresentation of an object that can be written as a sequence of\ncharacters on devices as a console, a file, or a pipe. The <A HREF=\"../Polyhedron_ref/Class_Polyhedron_incremental_builder_3#Enum_enum\">enum</A> <I><A HREF=\"Enum_Mode.html#Cross_link_anchor_1737\">Mode</A></I> distinguish between three different printing formats.\n<P>\n\nIn  <I><A HREF=\"Enum_Mode#Enum_Mode\">ASCII</A></I> mode, numbers \ne.g. the coordinates of a point or\nthe coefficients of a line, are written\nin a machine independent format. \nIn <A HREF=\"Enum_Mode#Enum_Mode\">BINARY</A> mode, data are written\nin a binary format, e.g. a double is represented\nas a sequence of four byte. The format depends on the machine.\n The mode  <A HREF=\"Enum_Mode#Enum_Mode\">PRETTY</A>\nserves mainly for debugging as the type of the geometric\nobject is written, as well as the data defining the object. For example\nfor a point at the origin with <A HREF=\"../Kernel_23_ref/Class_Cartesian.html#Cross_link_anchor_6\">Cartesian</A> double coordinates, the output\nwould be <I>PointC2(0.0, 0.0)</I>.  At the moment C<SMALL>GAL</SMALL> does not\nprovide input operations for pretty printed data.
