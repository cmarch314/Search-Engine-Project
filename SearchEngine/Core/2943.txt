David&nbsp;Umulis</div>\n    <div id=\"abstract\">\n      <img style=\"float:left;\" src=\"icon_drop.jpg\" alt=\"icon\">\n      The axial bodyplan of Drosophila melanogaster is determined during a process\ncalled morphogenesis. Shortly after fertilization, maternal bicoid mRNA is\ntranslated into Bicoid (Bcd). This protein establishes a spatially graded\nmorphogen distribution along the anterior-posterior (AP) axis of the embryo.\nBcd initiates AP axis determination by triggering expression of gap genes that\nsubsequently regulate each other's expression to form a precisely controlled\nspatial distribution of gene products. Reaction-diffusion models of gap gene\nexpression on a 1D domain have previously been used to infer complex genetic\nregulatory network (GRN) interactions by optimizing model parameters with\nrespect to 1D gap gene expression data. Here we construct a finite element\nreaction-diffusion model with a realistic 3D geometry fit to full 3D gap gene\nexpression data. Though gap gene products exhibit dorsal-ventral asymmetries,\nwe discover that previously inferred gap GRNs yield qualitatively correct AP\ndistributions on the 3D domain only when DV-symmetric initial conditions are\nemployed. Model patterning loses qualitative agreement with experimental data\nwhen we incorporate a realistic DV-asymmetric distribution of Bcd. Further, we\nfind that geometry alone is insufficient to account for DV-asymmetries in the\nfinal gap gene distribution. Additional GRN optimization confirms that the 3D\nmodel remains sensitive to GRN parameter perturbations. Finally, we find that\nincorporation of 3D data in simulation and optimization does not constrain the\nsearch space or improve optimization results.\n\n    </div>\n    <div id=\"bibtext\">\n      <h3>Download: <a href=\"/papers/Hengenius_PLOS1_2011/Hengenius_PLOS1_2011.pdf\">pdf</a></h3>\n      <h3>Text Reference</h3>\nJames Hengenius, Michael Gribskov, Ann Rundell, Charless Fowlkes, and David Umulis.\nAnalysis of gap gene reguation in a 3d organism-scale model of the drosophila melanogaster embryo.\n<em>PLoS ONE</em>, 6:e26797, 2011.<br>\n<h3>BibTeX Reference</h3>\n@article{<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hengenius_PLOS1_2011,<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;author = \"Hengenius, James and Gribskov, Michael and Rundell, Ann and Fowlkes, Charless and Umulis, David\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;journal = \"PLoS ONE\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;year = \"2011\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;issue = \"11\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;title = \"Analysis of Gap Gene Reguation in a 3D Organism-Scale Model of the Drosophila melanogaster Embryo\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;volume = \"6\",<br>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;pages = \"e26797\"<br>\n}<br>\n<br>\n    </div>\n  </div>\n  <div id=\"footer\">\n    <div>\n      <a href=\"/\">Computational Vision</a> |\n      <a href=\"http://www.ics.uci.edu/\">School of Information and Computer Sciences</a> |\n      <a href=\"http://www.uci.edu/\">UC Irvine</a>\n    </div>\n    <div id=\"updated\">&copy; 2007-2015 UC Irvine</div>\n  </div>\n  </div>\n</body>\n</html>\n\n", "id": 2943.0}
