It is similar to a vertical\n  version of <code>java.awt.FlowLayout</code> (which is strictly horizontal)\n  with 2 key exceptions:<br>\n  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It puts components in a single column, and will not\n  \"wrap\" the components when it reaches the bottom of the container.<br>\n  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is far more flexible for adjusting component\n  alignment.</p>\n  <p>There are methods to adjust the vertical alignment and the horizontal\n  alignment of all components in the container. The horizontal fill of the\n  components can also be adjusted, causing them to display at their\n  preferred widths, justifying to match the width of the widest component,\n  or filling the width of their parent container.  This makes it simple to\n  neatly aligned layouts.</p>\n  <p>Example:\n  <blockquote><pre>\nVerticalFlowLayout vfLayout = new pane = new JPanel(vfLayout);\npane.add(new JTextField(14);\npane.add(new JTextField(8);\npane.add(new JTextField(20);\n  </pre></blockquote>\n  Produces something like this:<br>\n  <img
