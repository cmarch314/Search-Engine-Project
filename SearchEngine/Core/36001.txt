Time has Twitter on Cover and <a href=\"http://www.time.com/time/printout/0,8816,1902604,00.html#\">a very nice article by Steven Johnson.</a>  I did not think that Twitter has any potential when I first heard about it &#8211; so following really resonated well with me:</p>\n<blockquote><p>The one thing you can say for certain about Twitter is that it makes a terrible first impression. You hear about this new service that lets you send 140-character updates to your &#8220;followers,&#8221; and you think, Why does the world need this, exactly? It&#8217;s not as if we were all sitting around four years ago scratching our heads and saying, &#8220;If only there were a technology that would allow me to send a message to my 50 friends, alerting them in real time about my choice of breakfast cereal.&#8221;</p></blockquote>\n<p>But once I started seeing some interesting applications I realized that it is a very unique thing, particularly:</p>\n<blockquote><p>Put those three elements together \u00e2\u20ac\u201d social networks, live searching and link-sharing \u00e2\u20ac\u201d and you have a cocktail that poses what may amount to the most interesting alternative to Google&#8217;s near monopoly in searching.</p></blockquote>\n<p>In fact, I am personally more interested in Twitter because it brings in the notion of an event as a package that can carry with it information as well as experience.  The link-sharing aspect of it along with the fact that time and location can be associated with it, makes it basically an &#8216;event&#8217; and the same sense that we have been using it in our group.  This is truly exciting.
