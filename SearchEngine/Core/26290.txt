The provisions of this federal law apply to the following areas:\n<P>\n- Structuring and use of the information resources, i. e. formation,\ncollection, editing, collation, retention, official search, dissemination\nand supply of documented information to the users,\n<P>\n- Formation and use of information techologies and corresponding\nprotective measures,\n<P>\n- Protection of information and the rights of subjects involved\nwith the information processes and informatisation.\n<P>\n2. This federal law has applications in the specified areas, so\nfar as they do not come under the provisions of the federal law\n&quot;On the Copyright and similar rights&quot;.\n<H4>Article 2 Definition of terms </H4>\n\n<P>\nTerms in the sense of this law: &quot;Information&quot; - Organisationally arranged document\nset together with information technologies, including computer-based\ntechniques, which execute the information processes;\n<P>\n&quot;Information resources&quot; - Individual documents and document\nsets, documents and document sets in the information systems (libraries,\narchives, collections, databases and other information collections);\n\n    </TD>\n<TD></TD>\n  </TR>\n\n<TR>\n<TD COLSPAN=\"2\"> \n\n<P>\n&quot;Information about citizens (personal data)&quot; (subsequently: &quot;Information\nsecurity facilities&quot;) - Programmed, technical, linguistic,\nlegal and organisational facilities (software, hardware and telecommunications,\ncomputer programs, technical dictionaries, thesauri and classifications,\ninstructions and methodologies, pragmatics, rules, plans and their\ndescriptions and other documentation), which emerge in the development\nor exploitation of the information systems and safeguard their\nability to function;\n<P>\n&quot;Owner (holder) of the information resources, information\nsystems, technologies and information security facilities&quot;\n- Any subject who may exercise the proprietary rights over these\nobjects without restriction;\n<P>\n&quot;Possessor of the information resources, information systems,\ntechnologies and information security facilities&quot; Government information resources of the Russian Federation\nare public; they are generally accessible. Exceptions relate to\nthe information resources in so far as they have been designated\nby law as for restricted access.\n<P>\n2. Documented information for restricted access arises from the\ninformation concerning state secrecy and from confidential information.\n<P>\n3. It is forbidden to qualify the following documents as information\nwith restricted access:\n<P>\n- Laws and other regulations which concern the legal position\nof the government bodies, territorial self-governing bodies, organisations\nand social associations, or the rights, freedoms and duties of\nthe citizens, and procedures involved;\n<P>\n- Documents which report on unusual events, ecological, meteorological,\ndemographic, health and epidemic-related facts, or contain other\ninformation which is of importance for the functioning of supply\nand production facilities or for the safety of the citizens and\nthe economy;\n<P>\n- Documents which report on activities of the government bodies\nand territorial self-governing bodies, on use of the budget funds\nor other governmental and local stocks, on economic situation\nand supply requirements, except for documents affecting state\nsecrets.\n<P>\n- Documents in the public collections of libraries and archives,\ninformation systems of government bodies, territorial self-governing\nbodies, social associations and organisations, which are of public\ninterest or essential for the exercise of citizens' rights, freedoms\nand duties.\n<P>\n4. The federal law &quot;On State Secrecy&quot; determines the\nsignificance of the information for state secrecy.\n<P>\n5. The decision about confidentiality of information is made on\nthe basis of the Russian Federation's legislation; Section 11\nof this federal law should be applied.\n<H4>Article 11 Information on citizens (personal data)</H4>\n\n<P>\n1. The nature of personal data which should be stored in the federal\ninformation resources, information resources of the territorial\nself-governing bodies and nongovernment organisations is specified\nby federal law.\n<P>\nPersonal data is regarded as confidential information. \n<P>\n<a name=\"article11\"></a>\nCollection, storage, usage and dissemination of information about\nprivate life, and processing of information which concerns personal\nand family secrecy, or encroaches upon post, telephone or telegram\nsecrecy or the secrecy of other forms of communication between\npersons is only permissible if a legal provision provides for\nthis, or the person affected has agreed.\n<P>\n2. Personal data may not be used to inflict economic or moral\ndamage on citizens, or to impede the exercise of the rights and\nfreedoms of the citizens of the Russian Federation. Restriction\nof the citizens' rights on the basis of information on social\norigin, race, nationality, language, religion or party membership\nis forbidden and is punished according to the law.\n<P>\n3. Natural and legal persons, who within their jurisdiction process,\ncollect or use personal data, bear the responsibility based on\nthe Russian Federal legislation for the violation of the provisions\nwhich govern the collection, processing and use of this information.\n<P>\n4. The activity of non-government organisations and private persons\nin the processing of personal information is compulsorily licensed.\nThe licensing procedure is specified in the Russian Federal legislation.\n<P>\n5. On application by the subjects acting according to Sections\n14 and 15 of this law and corresponding provisions of the law\non personal data, the illegality of the storage of personal data\nundertaken by the government bodies and organisations may be established\nin legal proceedings.\n<H3>Part 3 Use of the Information Resources</H3>\n\n<H4>Article 12 Realization of the access right to the information\ncontained in resources </H4>\n\n<P>\n1. The users - citizens, government bodies, territorial self-governing\nbodies, organisations and social associations - have the same\nrights at their disposal to the government information resources,\nand are not obliged to demonstrate the necessity of their requirement.\nThe exception is the restricted access information.\n<P>\nThe access of the natural and legal persons to the government\ninformation resources represents an important precondition for\nthe execution of social control of all government bodies, territorial\nself-governing bodies, social, political and other organisations;\nthe control of the economic and ecological situation and control\nin the other areas of community life are also made easier by this\nmeans.\n<P>\n2. According to the statutes, official regulations and information\nsupply agreements, the possessors of informational resources grant\ninformation users access to the information needed by them on\nthe basis of the applicable legislation.\n<P>\nInformation which the citizens and organisations obtain from the\ngovernment information resources may be used for preparing commercial\ninformation; information altered in this way may only be disseminated\nafterwards with references to its origin.\n<P>\nThe source of profit results in this case from the aggregate of\nwork used in creating the commercial information and applied resources,\nbut the emergence of the information from the information resources\nis unavoidable.\n<P>\n3. Information access arrangements (access location and time,\npersons responsible, obligatory procedures) are specified by the\nowners or possessors of the information resources; these are obliged\nto take into account the legal conditions.\n<P>\nDirectories of information and information services, as well as\ninformation about the procedures and conditions of access to information,\nare available to the users free of charge.\n<P>\n4. Government bodies and other organisations responsible for the\nstructuring and use of the information resources assure operative\nand unimpeded access to the documented information for those who\nneed it, according to the duties specified in their statutes.\n<P>\n5. The way of collecting and storing documented information with\nrestricted access, and the protection rules and conditions of\nuse relating to this, are given by the government bodies who are\nresponsible for certain types of information or information sets.\nThey may also be specified directly by the information owner according\nto the legal provisions.\n<H4>Article 13 Granting access to information </H4>\n\n<P>\n<a name=\"article13_1\"></a>\n1. Government bodies and territorial self-governing bodies are\nobliged to establish generally accessible information collections,\nwhich store information about their area of operation and those\nof their subordinate organisations.\n<P>\nWithin their jurisdiction they also guarantee public access to\nthe information about citizens' freedoms and duties, and about\ntheir safety; and also to the other information which is important\nfrom the point of view of the public interest.\n<P>\n2. The processors of the documented information concerning the\ncitizens are obliged to make the information available to those\nconcerned without charge. Limitations of this liability are only\npermitted under the statutory conditions.\n<P>\n3. Subjects who surrender information concerning themselves according\nto sections 7 and 8 of this law, for the purpose of forming the\ninformation resources, are entitled to make use of this information\nfree of charge.\n<P>\n4. If the holder of information resources refuses the person concerned\naccess to the information concerning him, this person can bring\nproceedings in court.\n<H4>Article 15 Obligations and liabilities of the holder of information\nresources</H4>\n\n<P>\n1. Holders of information resources are obliged to satisfy the\nprocessing conditions and information access rules determined\nby the legislation of the Russian Federation or by the owner of\nthe information resource.\n<P>\n2. Holders of information resources are liable for the violation\nof the information procedure envisaged in the legislation of the\nRussian Federation.\n<H3>Part 5 Protection of information and subjects' rights in the\ncourse of the information and informatisation processes</H3>\n\n<H4>Article 20 Aim of the protection </H4>\n\n<P>\nAim of the protection is:\n<P>\n- to prevent the decrease, theft, loss and falsifying of information;\n\n<P>\n- to remove dangers to the security of persons, society and the\nstate; \n<P>\n- to prevent unauthorised destruction, changing, falsifying, copying,\nlocking and other illegal forms of intervention in the information\nresources and information systems;\n<P>\n- to secure rights of ownership to documented information; \n<P>\n- to grant constitutional rights of citizens to privacy and confidentiality\nof personal data in the information systems;\n<P>\n- to grant state secrecy and confidentiality of the documented\ninformation according to the applicable legislation;\n<P>\n- to protect rights of subjects in the course of information processes\nand in the design, manufacture and use of information systems,\ntechnologies and information security facilities.\n<H4>Article 21 Information protection </H4>\n\n<P>\n1. All documented information, unauthorised access to which could\nlead to damage for owners, possessors, or other persons, is protected.\n<P>\nThe information protection regime should be established for individual\ncategories of information by the following subjects:\n<P>\n- Responsible bodies, named in the federal law &quot;On State\nSecrecy&quot; to determine the corresponding protection order\nfor information affecting state secrecy\n<P>\n- The holders of information resources, or their representatives,\naccording to the present federal law, in the case of the confidential\ninformation.\n<P>\nThe provisions of this law determine the information protection\nfor the personal data.\n<P>\n2. Government bodies and organisations which are responsible for\nthe structuring and use of the information resources which require\nprotection, and similarly bodies and organisations which produce\nand use information systems and information technologies for processing\nrestricted access information, are subject to the legislation\nof the Russian Federation in their activity.\n<P>\n3. The government bodies exercise control of information protection,\nof the corresponding software and hardware resources and also\nof the organisational rules by which the restricted access information\nis processed in the information systems. This control is executed\naccording to the rules determined by the government of the Russian\nFederation.\n<P>\n4. Organisations which process government-owned restricted access\ninformation are also obliged to set up special information protection\nservices.\n<P>\n5. The holders of information resources, or the persons authorised\nby them, can check the observance of the information protection\nmeasures, and forbid or suspend information processing if the\nprotection rules are not observed.\n<P>\n6. The owner or possessor of the documented information may approach\nthe government bodies to discover whether his information is processed\nin observance of the information protection rules. Appropriate\nmonitoring bodies are named by the government of the Russian Federation.\nThese bodies are obliged themselves to observe the conditions\nof information confidentiality, and regard monitoring results\nas confidential.\n<H4>Article 22 Rights and obligations of the subjects in the area\nof information protection </H4>\n\n<P>\n1. The owner of documents, document sets and information systems\nor the persons authorised by him draw up, in accordance with the\ncorresponding federal law, rules on information usage. The location,\ntime, persons responsible and the processing procedure are specified\nin these rules.\n<P>\n2. The possessors of documents, document sets and information\nsystems secure a qualifying information protection level according\nto the legislation of the Russian Federation.\n<P>\n3. The risk involved in the use of non-certified information systems\nand non-certified information protection facilities is borne by\ntheir holders.\n<P>\nUse of information taken from non-licensed information systems\nis at the information user's risk.\n<P>\n4. The holder of documents, document sets and information systems\nmay approach organisations concerned with certification, and request\na check of the information protection in the information resources\nand information systems belonging to him; he may also request\nadvice.\n<P>\n5. The possessor of the documents, document sets and information\nsystems is obliged to inform the owner of the information resources\nand/or information systems about all violations of the information\nprotection provisions.\n<H4>Article 23 Protection of subjects' rights in the area of information\nprocesses and information </H4>\n\n<P>\n1. The granting of subjects' rights in the structuring and use\nof information resources, in designing, producing and using information\nsystems, technologies and information protection facilities should\nprotect against violations of law and illegal dealings, contribute\nto the restoration of the legal system and secure the compensation\nof those affected.\n<P>\n2. The courts, arbitration tribunals and arbitration commissions\nimplement the protection of the subjects in the given areas, taking\ninto account the peculiarities of violations of law and damages\nthereby arising.\n<P>\n3. Responsibility for violations of law which can arise in dealing\nwith the documented information is borne by the government bodies,\norganisations and responsible persons according to the legislation\nof the Russian Federation and the legislation of its subjects.\n<P>\nFor the purpose of investigating the conflict situations and granting\npersonal rights, in the structuring of information resources,\nsetting up and use of information systems, technologies and information\nprotection facilities, arbitration tribunals can be formed for\na limited period or permanently.\n<P>\nArbitration tribunals decide upon conflicts and settle disputes\nsubmitted to them. They are guided by the procedural order which\nwas determined by legislation for the procedures of arbitration\ntribunals.\n<P>\n4.
