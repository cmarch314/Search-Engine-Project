it &#8212; somebody has decided to say that the Emperor has no clothes.</p>\n<p><a title=\"wisdom\" href=\"http://news.com.com/Taking+measure+of+the+cult+of+the+amateur/2010-1025_3-6173903.html?tag=html.alert\">New.com reports</a> about a new book by Andrew Keen.</p>\n<blockquote><p>In a deliciously interesting part of the article is</p>\n<blockquote><p>The subtitle of his book states his thesis bluntly: &#8220;How the democratization of the digital world is assaulting our economy, our culture, and our values.&#8221;</p>\n<p>Them be fighting words, to be sure, and Keen is being purposely provocative. But he&#8217;s worth reading. Keen&#8217;s not writing from the uninformed point of view of a technophobe. In his previous life, he was the founder of Audiocafe.com. That said, he&#8217;s not at all happy about where things are headed, bemoaning the advent of &#8220;an endless digital forest of mediocrity&#8221; as the number of new blogs doubles each six months.</p></blockquote>\n<p>This is true but society always goes thru such phases.\u00c2\u00a0 What is needed are strong mechanisms that can do prospecting and filtering of all this data to find what
