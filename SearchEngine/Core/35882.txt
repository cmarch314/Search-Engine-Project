predictors for predicting\nsecondary structure, beta-contacts, and tertiary structure of Transmembrane\nBeta-Barrel (TMB) proteins. TMB proteins are embedded\nin the outer membranes of mitochondria, Gram-negative bacteria\nand chloroplasts. These proteins perform critical functions, including\nactive ion-transport and passive nutrient intake. Therefore, there is a\nneed for accurate prediction of secondary and tertiary structure of\nTMB proteins. Traditional homology modeling methods, however, fail\non most TMB proteins since very few non-homologous TMB structures\nhave been determined.
