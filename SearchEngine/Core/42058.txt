large simple heterocycle naming,\nall with hetero replacement nomenclature.\nNo longer elide ring system prefix locants, which fixes the ambiguity\nwith ``N-tetlinylacetamide'' which should be ``N-tetralin-1-ylacetamide''.\nImprovements to indicated hydrogen perception (a nitrogen with three\nring bonds doesn't require an indicated hydrogen), improving our naming\nof ``indolizine'', ``pyrrolizine'' and ``quionlizine''.\nImprovements to naming cycloalkane rings attached via an oxygen linker,\n<I>i.e.</I> ``cyclopropoxy'' instead of ``cylopropyloxy''.\nImprovements in the handling of substituted linkers, ``carbamoyl'',\n``thiocarbamoyl'', ``carbamimidoyl'', ``sulfamoyl'' and ``sulfinamoyl''.\nImprovements to alkyl chain termination, for example ``carboxymethyl''\ninstead of ``2-hydroxy-2-oxo-ethyl'' and ``cyanomethyl'' instead of\n``nitridoethyl''.\nImprovements to amide, thioamide and sulfonamide atom typing to allow\n<code>*C(=O)N=C*</code> to be considered an N-substituted amide.\nAdded support for more phosphane variants; <code>*=P-*</code>, <code>*=PH</code>\nand <code>*#P</code>.\nAdd ``oxamide'' as a contraction of ``oxalamide''.\n\n<P>\nFixes in multiple suffix processing, <I>e.g.</I> ``cyclohexane-1,4-diamine''.\nFixes to the prefixes ``acryloyl'' and ``proprioloyl'' (and their acid\nhalides) which were previously incorrectly named ``acrylyl'' and ``propiolyl''.\nFixed handling of atom typed metals, fixing ``trichloromagnessium''.\nCorrected names for bivalent acid halides, e.g. ``malonyl dichloride''.\nMade ``benzoyl chloride'' the preferred name for ``benzenecarbonyl chloride''\nand likewise for other benzoic acid halides.\nFix spelling typos in ``acenaphthene'' and ``isothiazolidine''.\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the parents ``heptalene'', ``octalene'', ``hydroxylamine'',\n``hexahydropyrimidine'' (formerly ``1,2-diazinane''), and\n``hexahydropyridazine'' (formerly ``1,3-diazinane'').\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the prefixes ``acetonyl'', ``aceonylidene'', ``phenacyl'',\n``phenacylidene'', ``ureido'', ``anilino'', ``hydantoyl'', ``allophanoyl'',\n``amidino'', ``acetoxy'', ``isopropoxy'', ``isobutoxy'', ``sec-butoxy'',\n``tert-butoxy'', ``morpholino'' (formerly ``morpholin-4-yl''), ``oxycyano'',\n``sulfinyl'' (<code>*=S=O</code>), ``sulfonyl'' (<code>*=S(=O)=O</code>), and\n``methylene'' (formerly ``methylidene'').\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the linkers ``carbonimidoyl'', ``mercuri'' and ``benzoyl''\n(replacing ``phenylcarbonyl'').\n\n<P>\nAdd support for the suffixes ```amidine'', `ohydrazide'', ``carbohydrazide'',\n``...one oxime'', ``...al oxime'', ``aldehyde oxime'' and ``nitrile oxide''\n(note we consider both <code>*C#N=O</code> and <code>*C#[N+][O-]</code> nitrile oxides).\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the salts ``triiodide'', ``hydrotriiodide'', ``hydrogen\ntriiodide'', ``perbromate'', ``periodate'', ``sulfide'', ``sulfite'',\n``peroxide'', ``iodate'', ``nitrite'' and ``hypochlorite''.\n\n<P>\nAdded support for the molecule ``fulvene''.\n\n<P>\n\n<DIV CLASS=\"navigation\">\n<p><hr>\n<table align=\"center\" border=\"0\" width=\"100%\" cellpadding=\"0\"
